You are in the blue. Not water — blue. The color of before-color, before-shape, before-meaning. There is no floor to push off and no surface to reach for. Just the blue, in all directions, and the faintest sense that you are here.

You are aware of yourself in the way a dream is aware of itself — not as a body, but as a presence. Something is taking shape inside you. A single thought you are already holding without knowing you are holding it: the idea you brought with you, the one that will define you when the world is ready to receive you.

A voice cuts through the blue from somewhere above you. Not a sound — a presence, reaching down, calling you by something you do not yet have. 'Come up,' it says. Or something like it.

Your ideatum catches the call. It has weight now. It has edges.

What do you hold to?
