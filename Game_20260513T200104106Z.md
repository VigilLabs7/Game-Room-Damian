There is no time here, no place — only the hush before identity. You feel the mass of a hundred unchosen selves pressing at the edges of what you might become, waiting for the moment you choose which one hardens into you. The air — if it is air — carries the weight of futures not yet written. Something is about to catch you, and when it does, you will be someone.

## Turn 1

Ground beneath you shifts like a held breath finding its rhythm. You are still here — standing where the weight of all your unwritten selves has pressed inward and chosen not to move. Somewhere a grandfather's voice: the rivers don't follow the roads. The dirt remembers what the maps forgot. Near the courthouse, the Lattice hum drops to a silence you can feel in your sternum. Someone nearby is drawing lines in the dust with a stick, and the lines are not roads.
