The blue is not water. You have been in water — you remember it, or you imagine you do — and this is not it. There is no surface to break toward, no floor to push off from, no direction that means anything. You are suspended in something without edges, and the something is the color of the space between when there is nothing there. You feel yourself knowing you are here, and that is the only solid thing you have.

There is no body yet. Not really. There is the idea of a body — the memory of having had one, or the shape of one you might have — and the blue fills the space where the body would be. You are not alone. You know there are others somewhere, in the same suspension, in the same blue, but they cannot be reached and you cannot reach them. The rules here do not include meeting.

You are holding something. Not with hands — you do not have hands yet — but with the part of you that will become a will, that will become a self. A single idea. Your ideatum.
