The blue has no bottom. You drift in it and the blue is all there is — above, below, beside. No light reaches here that you could call a sun. No sound arrives that you could call a voice. You are aware of yourself the way you might be aware of a shape in fog: present, but not yet solid.

Something is taking shape in you. Not a memory — you don't have those yet. Not a name. What you have is a single thought, small and bright, catching in the nothing like a finger on a thread. You grip it without knowing you are gripping anything.

Then: a voice. From somewhere outside the blue. Calling.

Come up, it says. Come up, darling.

Something in you answers. You don't know what it is yet. But it answers.


## Turn 1

**Player:** I grip harder. The voice is wrong — too sweet, trying too hard to mean something it doesn't. I push down instead of up. The blue thickens, resists, and then gives the way water gives when you find the cold current underneath. I sink toward it. Whatever is calling from above, I want to see what it's calling from.

**Game Master:**

You push down and the blue gives. Not all at once — it thickens around you first, resists, and then parts the way deep water does when you find the cold current running beneath the surface. You sink.

The voice above doesn't stop. Come up, darling, it says again, and the sweetness in it is the problem — it is too smooth, too practiced, the kindness of someone who has something to sell. You have no memory of being sold to. You have no memory of anything. But you know this: the voice is trying too hard to mean something, and whatever is trying this hard should be watched, not answered.

You sink past the place where the voice reaches. The blue here is different — denser, older, the blue before the blue. Your ideatum catches on something as you pass through: a shape that is not a shape, a weight that is not a body. The concept takes form around the word you don't have yet. *Duty.* Not your duty — the duty of the thing watching from above. It has a duty toward you and it is performing that duty with a sweetness you do not trust.

The blue thins slightly below you. Not much. But enough to know there is a direction down, and that direction is yours to take.
