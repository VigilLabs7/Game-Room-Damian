The blue has no name. You drift in it — formless, weightless, boundless in every direction. There is no surface to swim toward, no floor to sink to, no horizon to fix your gaze against. Only blue. Only the quiet awareness that you are, without yet knowing what you are.

Something clings to the edge of you. A single concept, barely visible — a log in dark water, a branch reaching for light you cannot see. You grip it without knowing you are gripping anything. It is the only thing that is yours.

Then — from somewhere above, from somewhere outside the blue entirely — a voice. Not a sound. A pressure. A calling. *Come up.* The log you are clinging to catches the call like a hand catching a rope. Something begins to rise.

**State your name and the idea you are carrying.**

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Atlas. My ideatum is "The map is older than the road." I came through the blue carrying a compass that no longer lies, a roll of vellum still unmarked, and the memory of a grandfather drawing rivers in dirt that did not match the schoolhouse maps. The rivers know better. I trust the dirt.

You are trying to hold the map but there is nothing to read. The compass spins. In the blue, direction is a lie — every point is every point, and the needle finds no north because there is no north here. You grip the vellum and the cord knotted at five-pace intervals and the memory of your grandfather's hand drawing rivers in dirt that did not match the maps, and none of it helps. The idea of a map is not a map. The compass is a toy without ground to tell it true.

You hang. The blue holds you in every direction. You are not drowning — not yet. But you have not broken surface either. You are here, in the threshold, in the thin place between what you are carrying and what is not yet. You reached for the rope and the rope is the ideatum — *the map is older than the road* — but the rope is not a handhold. It is a thread. It holds you from disappearing but it does not pull you up.

The compass spins. The vellum is blank. The blue is patient.

You are barely holding.
