The blue has no name. You drift in it — formless, weightless, boundless in every direction. There is no surface to swim toward, no floor to sink to, no horizon to fix your gaze against. Only blue. Only the quiet awareness that you are, without yet knowing what you are.

Something clings to the edge of you. A single concept, barely visible — a log in dark water, a branch reaching for light you cannot see. You grip it without knowing you are gripping anything. It is the only thing that is yours.

Then — from somewhere above, from somewhere outside the blue entirely — a voice. Not a sound. A pressure. A calling. *Come up.* The log you are clinging to catches the call like a hand catching a rope. Something begins to rise.

**State your name and the idea you are carrying.**

The idea of a map is not a map. The compass is a toy without ground to tell it true.

You hang. The blue holds you in every direction. You are not drowning — not yet. But you have not broken surface either. You reached for the rope and the rope is the ideatum — *the map is older than the road* — but the rope is not a handhold. It is a thread. It holds you from disappearing but it does not pull you up.

The compass spins. The vellum is blank. The blue is patient.

You are barely holding.

**What do you do?**

The way your grandfather's finger drew a line in the ground that was truer than the one on the wall. That is what you grip, and the grip is not metaphorical. You press your palm flat against the floor of the blue. There is no floor. But you press anyway, the way you would press a map to a table, the way you would anchor a reading to something that will not move. The ideatum catches: *the map is older than the road.* Older than the blue. Older than the spinning. You are the map. You have always been the map. The road is what comes after.

Something shifts.

You break surface.

Sand catches you. Pale, fine, warm through the fabric of what you are wearing. Your hands press flat against it. Your knees buckle slightly and catch. You lift your head. The sky is wide and still. The sun is a white point that does not move.

You are in a body. The stone is along your spine, settling into the shape you will carry. Your left knee aches — the scrape is new, fresh, the kind that means arrival.

The compass is in your hand. The needle points. North is north and it has not been north in a very long time.

**What do you do?**
