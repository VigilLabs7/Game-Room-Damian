The blue has no bottom. The blue has no surface. You hang in it and the hanging is all there is — no up to kick toward, no down to sink toward, only the same submergence in every direction, the same unending pale depthless color pressing against awareness like water against a diver's ears. Somewhere in the blue a single thought is forming. Not a word yet. Not a name. Something you carried here — a concept, a purpose, the germ of a self that does not yet have a shape it can hold. The blue is patient. It has been waiting. You have been drifting.

A voice cuts through from somewhere outside the blue. Not beside you. Above. *Come up.* Or something like it — the words arrive before the sound, pressing against the part of you that is still being built. Your ideatum catches the call like a hand catching a rope. Something in the blue begins to thin.

You are not yet who you will be. But you are about to become.

The cracked teapot is the only solid thing. You hold it and it holds you.

The blue thins at the edges. Not enough to see through. Enough to know there is a surface above, and beyond the surface something else, and the something else is where the mendable world lives. You do not know what broke you. You know it can be fixed.
