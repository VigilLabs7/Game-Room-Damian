You are in the blue.

Not blue like a sky or a lake — blue like a word spoken in a dark room, filling every space at once. There is no surface above you, no floor below, no sound except the complete and absolute quiet of a place where nothing has ever needed to speak. You are aware of yourself the way a word is aware of its own meaning: not as a body, not as a location, but as the bare fact of existing at all.

You drift. There is nothing else to do.

Somewhere — not near, not far, not in any direction you could point to — something is taking shape. A single concept, still soft at the edges, still too new to name. It is the only thing here that is becoming instead of simply being. You are holding it without knowing you are holding it.

The blue has no end and no bottom. You have no edges. But something in you is beginning to insist.

What are you holding onto?
