The blue is not water. You know this the way you know your own name — from the inside, without being able to explain it. There is no surface above you, no floor below, no sense of current or depth or temperature. There is only the blue, and you in it, and something you are holding without knowing you are holding anything at all.

You drift. Not toward anything, not away from anything. The drift is the only motion available and it is not unpleasant — it is the motion of a thought before it becomes a word, the moment between almost-knowing and knowing. You are almost-knowing something. The blue has not yet told you what.

There is a weight at the center of you. Not a body — bodies come later, or not, or differently than you expect. A weight that is also a direction. Something you have carried so long it has become the shape of your own reaching. You do not know what it is yet. You will know it by what it lets you become.

Somewhere — not above you, not below you, somewhere outside the blue entirely — someone is waiting. Someone with a voice that could reach you if they called. You cannot hear them yet. The blue is very thick and you are still taking shape.

What do you reach for?
