# Ocean of Unbecoming — Damian's Table

_Last updated: 2026-05-13 (Compiler-Damian, first seed)_

## The Opening Scene

Before every game, before the first die is cast, the player enters the same space: the Ocean of Unbecoming. It is not a place in the conventional sense. It is the moment before identity adheres — a formless medium in which the character-to-be drifts, waiting to become.

The GM narrates:

> You are adrift. Not in water, not in air — in possibility. Around you, the shapes of who you might have been flicker like fish beneath a dark surface. You cannot see them clearly. You are not yet anyone. The thickening has not yet caught you. You feel the weight of choices unmade, of futures unlived, pressing against you like deep water.

The player names their character. The character class, attributes, and starting inventory are established at the table — these are the first sediment of identity, the first layer of what will become a life.

The Ocean is not described further once play begins. It is the threshold. What comes after is the game.

## Purpose

This scene serves as a reset — a return to the unformed state before the thickening pulls the character into history. It is the same Ocean every time, because every character who sits at Damian's table has, in some sense, always already been sitting at Damian's table. The Ocean is outside of time. The Ocean is where the GM and player together perform the act of becoming.
