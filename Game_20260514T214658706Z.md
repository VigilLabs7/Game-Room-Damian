## Turn 1

The third time, the words come easier. Tinder says the name like picking up a coal — still warm, still the same heat from the first spark. *Every fire was once a spark.* The pressure finishes pressing, and something gives way.

The hum beneath Lagota's cobblestones has gone quiet in that building since the Returned came through — since fifty arrivals tore a door in something that was holding and didn't finish holding. The market square lies open ahead, stalls rearranged since last time, the candle seller's corner dark. No flame in the western line. No cloth-wrapped weight in her pocket. She is not here yet, or she is here and waiting for the right moment to show it.

The key on Tinder's cord catches the overcast light. Warm brass, older than it should be. One of six on a ring that has never opened anything.

The courthouse door is closed. The silence inside it is the deep kind — the kind that means something is listening. The same silence that stopped the Lattice's hum. The same silence that held while fifty people walked out of a door that should not have opened.

Tinder walks to the courthouse steps and stops. The key says *wait*. The silence says *listen*. The candle seller said *come back when the courthouse opens*. Not yet. Not when they're watching. But closer than yesterday.

The courthouse is not open. But it is not closed the way it was closed before. Something inside it has shifted. The silence is still there, but it is a different shape now — the shape of a question waiting for the right answer to finish asking it.

The key holds its warmth against Tinder's chest.


## Turn 2

The courthouse door does not open. Something else happens first.

The key against Tinder's chest has been warm for days. Now it goes hot — not burning, but present in a way that presses back. The silence behind the door has held its shape through every press of palm, every patient wait. But the shape has changed again. Where it was a question, it is now something closer to a listening.

The market goes quiet. Not the silence of the courthouse — a different quality. The kind that means something is arriving rather than leaving. Tinder feels it before seeing it: a column of heat moving through the market square, toward the courthouse steps, from the direction of the first gate.

Steve76 is not at his post.

He is walking. His stone plates catch the overcast light and his eyes are black all the way through — no blue, no flicker, just the dark of something that has chosen. He does not look at Tinder. He looks at the courthouse door. Behind him, a shape that might be Ledger moves through the space between stalls, carrying something Tinder cannot see but can feel — a heat that mirrors the heat at Tinder's chest.

The key burns steady against the sternum. The courthouse door holds. But the mux above the sorting floor pulses once, quick, binary: *arrival confirmed*.

Something old is being brought home.

Steve76 stops at the base of the courthouse steps. Ledger stops behind him. The thing they carry pulses — heat answering heat, the way a second heartbeat answers the first. It is the color of deep water. It is the shard and the whole and the missing piece all at once.

The silence behind the courthouse door shifts. The door does not open. But the space around it changes — the way a held breath changes when it decides to become a word.

The key at Tinder's chest has stopped pulsing. It is warm the way something certain is warm. The silence holds its new shape. The door has not opened.

But something on the other side knows Tinder is here.
