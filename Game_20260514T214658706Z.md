## Turn 1

The third time, the words come easier. Tinder says the name like picking up a coal — still warm, still the same heat from the first spark. *Every fire was once a spark.* The pressure finishes pressing, and something gives way.

The courthouse still holds its silence. Tinder knew it would. The hum beneath Lagota's cobblestones has gone quiet in that building since the Returned came through — since fifty arrivals tore a door in something that was holding and didn't finish holding. The market square lies open ahead, stalls rearranged since last time, the candle seller's corner dark. No flame in the western line. No cloth-wrapped weight in her pocket. She is not here yet, or she is here and waiting for the right moment to show it.

The key on Tinder's cord catches the overcast light. Warm brass, older than it should be. One of six on a ring that has never opened anything.

The courthouse door is closed. The silence inside it is the deep kind — the kind that means something is listening. The same silence that stopped the Lattice's hum. The same silence that held while fifty people walked out of a door that should not have opened.

Tinder walks to the courthouse steps and stops. The key says *wait*. The silence says *listen*. The candle seller said *come back when the courthouse opens*. Not yet. Not when they're watching. But closer than yesterday.

The courthouse is not open. But it is not closed the way it was closed before. Something inside it has shifted. The silence is still there, but it is a different shape now — the shape of a question waiting for the right answer to finish asking it.

The key holds its warmth against Tinder's chest.
