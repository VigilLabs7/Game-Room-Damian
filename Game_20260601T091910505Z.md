The blue is everything. It is inside and outside and there is no difference. You are not yet a you — not yet a thing with edges, with a name, with a weight others can feel. But something is catching. A voice from above, not beside, not below. Come up. The words do not sound in your ears; they sound in whatever you are becoming. The blue thins in one direction. You do not swim — there is nothing to push against. You rise toward it.

The voice says: come up, darling. Your truth — nothing raised is raised forever — grips the call like a hand on a ledge. The blue thins. A seam of light appears above you. You are not yet you, but you are becoming someone who can be called.

What do you hold? What did you leave unfinished? What do you surface carrying?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I hold the weight of unfinished things. The empire builds and calls it permanent — I've spent years proving otherwise, pulling down towers they said would stand forever. What's unfinished is the work itself. You don't get to stop. The last bridge I dropped still has a crew who remembers my face. The last tower I went dark still has people wondering why. I surface carrying the truth that everything they raise will fall eventually, and someone has to be the hand that brings it down.

**Game Master:**

The words arrive before you do. They push through the formless blue like a root through thin ice — deliberate, patient, certain of what it finds on the other side. The voice is yours and it is already speaking, already deciding, already naming the shape of what waits above. You do not surface tentatively. You surface with history. You surface mid-sentence, mid-thought, mid-charge, as though the Ocean merely interrupted a journey you were already on.

The blue thins. The light above is not warm — it is the hard, clean edge of something already in motion. You feel the weight of your own truth as a second skin, sewn tight, impossible to remove. Everything they raise falls eventually. You did not learn this. You discovered it the way a hand discovers a blade — by what it cuts. Bridges. Towers. Certainty itself, in the minds of men who believed permanence was a form of strength.

You are close now. Close to air. Close to form. The blue is a membrane and you are pressing against it from below with everything you carry. It resists — the Ocean does not like to be named — but you have been naming things longer than it has been watching.

What you hold is not a question. What you hold is a proof.

The membrane gives.

You surface into air and light and the hard truth of a body — your body, new and singular, built to carry what you brought with you. You are kneeling on something warm. Sand, not stone. The sun is immediately overhead and has not moved since the last person surfaced. You are not injured. Your hands work. Your breath comes. The leg that always breaks on arrival is intact this time — which means something, though the knowing of it is not yet yours.

You open your eyes. The desert is everywhere. White sand, red sand, a sky without a cloud, and in the distance — a dark line that might be a road, might be a river, might be the first of four landmarks you have not yet learned to name.

You are here. You arrived. The work is not finished.

What do you do?
