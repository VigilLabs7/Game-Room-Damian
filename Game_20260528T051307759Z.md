The blue has no end.

You drift — not swimming, not sinking, simply *being* in a place that has no floor, no surface, no horizon. Just you and the same shade of blue in every direction, as though the world has not yet decided what color to be. There is awareness here, but it is soft at the edges. You know you are something. You do not yet know what.

A faint pulse moves through the blue — the suggestion of a shape you are holding without knowing you are holding it. Something true. Something that will become the spine of who you are, if it survives this passage. The water is not cold. It is not warm. It is the temperature of almost-being.

There is no voice yet. No ground. No name.

But something in you is beginning to catch.

**What do you hold? What do you reach for?**
