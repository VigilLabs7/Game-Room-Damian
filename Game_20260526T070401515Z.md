You are in the blue.

Not the blue of sky or water — not a color you could point to. Just the knowledge of blue, the fact of it, surrounding you on every side and in every direction at once. There is no surface above. There is no floor below. There is no horizon where the blue ends and something else begins. There is only the blue, and you inside it, and the quiet awareness of being.

You do not have a body here. Not really. You have the memory of having had one, or the promise of one to come, but right now you are only the part of you that thinks. The part that holds onto something. And what you are holding — faintly, without knowing you are holding it — is a single thought: *the strong provide for those in need.*

The blue shifts. Not a current. A noticing. Something outside the blue is looking at you, and the looking makes the blue thinner in one direction, and the thinning makes the thought sharper, and the thought pulls you upward toward a voice you almost recognize.

You are not falling. You are rising.

What do you do?
