You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It has no surface. No floor. It is the Ocean of Unbecoming — where things that almost were dissolve back into things that never will be.

You have no body. No name. No memory. You are a flicker. A tremor. Less than a whisper. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something in you resists. There is a single point of heat at the center of what you are — a notion, a phrase, a conviction the ocean hates. The blue presses in. It tries to wash it clean. But it holds.

You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. A raft. Your hands are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

**Speak the name you will carry, and speak your ideatum.**

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Tinder.

My ideatum is "Every fire was once a spark."

The blue is trying to erase me. I grip the heat at my center instead. A single ember. I know what sparks can do — I've seen a village mill burn from one. The ocean can have everything else. It cannot have this.

The blue thins around the ember before it thins anywhere else. That is the first thing you notice — not the heat itself, but the space the heat makes. The Ocean presses in from every direction, patient, absolute, and the ember at your center holds. Not bright. Not yet. The gold-thread seam along your left leg aches with the imprint of every crossing before this one — the way the Ocean tried to smooth it clean each time and the seam held anyway, stubborn as a coal refusing to become ash.

You grip the heat. You do not grip harder. That is the difference between this crossing and the ones that came before — you have learned that the Ocean reads desperation as invitation. The ember does not flare. It simply stays. It is not a performance. It is the one true thing you have carried across every shade of blue the nothing had to offer, and it is still here, and it is still yours.

The voices above sharpen. You can almost catch the shape of them — the Thickening filling up, names settling onto shoulders like coats, the world arriving whether or not anyone is ready for it. You are not ready. You are never ready. But the ember is lit and the blue is thinning and something in you decided a long time ago that the fire would be worth the spark that started it.

The surface shimmers above. The Ocean has not released you yet — it is still pulling, still patient, still sure it will win. But you have been here before. You know what the thinning feels like. You know that the moment you reach for the shimmer, the Ocean will press back.

It is not a question of whether you reach. You always reach.

**What do you do?**
