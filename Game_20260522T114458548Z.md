The blue holds you.

Not sky-blue, not sea-blue — something before those categories had names. You drift in it, and the drifting is all there is. No surface above, no floor below, no sound except the silence that exists when nothing has yet decided what it's for.

The compass in your pocket — if you have one — points to nothing. The mend against your chest — if there is one — reads nothing yet. You are pre-language. Pre-history. The moment before the moment has a name.

But you will find a name. The blue always lets go eventually.

What are you holding onto?
