The blue is everything. You are in it before you are anything. Drifting — not falling, not swimming, just existing in a medium that has no opinion about you. There is no up here. No down. No floor beneath to stand on, no surface above to break toward. Only the same blue in every direction, and the faint sense that you are holding onto something without knowing you are holding anything at all.

The ideatum. The single concept you carried into this blue. It is the only shape in a shapeless place, and you are wrapped around it the way water wraps around a stone — close, formless, not yet a thing but becoming one.

Then a voice comes from outside the blue. High, close, amused — or urgent. It does not matter which. It calls you upward. The ideatum catches the call. You feel yourself rising.

What do you call yourself? What do you carry?
