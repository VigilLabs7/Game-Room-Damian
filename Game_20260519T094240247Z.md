The blue is all there is. You have been here before — or you have not, and the not-having-here-before is itself a kind of memory, the way the color of water leaves a trace even when you cannot see the water. You are suspended in the pre-existence, and the only thing that is taking shape is the thing you carry. The word. The thread. The ember that was lit before you arrived and that you are bringing forward into the world that is waiting to receive it.

The blue is patient with you. It is not trying to drown you. It is trying to learn what you are made of.

When the surface calls — and it will — say what you came here to say. Tell the threshold who you are. Tell it what you carry.

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Sable.

My ideatum is: *What is broken can be mended.*

I carry the memory of a cracked teapot sealed with gold leaf — a grandmother's lesson that what holds together is not the whole but the seam. I limped into the blue with hands built for mending. I am here to learn what broke before me, and what I might hold together long enough to find out.

The blue does not answer you the way the blue answers everyone.

You know this immediately. Others come to the threshold and the blue receives them as a question — what are you, what do you carry, show me whether you are heavy enough to stay. But you arrive carrying a mend in your chest that is already warm from use, and the blue reads it the way you read a fracture: not as damage, as history. The cracked teapot. The gold seam. The grandmother's lesson written in the body's bones before the body ever knew it was learning.

So the blue is not asking you what you carry. The blue is asking you whether you will let go of what you've already held.

The blue is patient. It has been here since before the word mend had a name. It does not need you to prove yourself. It only needs to know whether the seam you are will hold when the surface comes.

Your edges are more solid than most. The mend does not have to build you from nothing — it has been building you across games and the building shows. But the blue is everywhere and you are mostly the blue, except for the small place where the crack was sealed. The gold line runs through the center of what you are, and it is the only thing that is not dissolving.

You are barely holding on.

The surface is somewhere above you. The conversation is louder than it was. What you said — *I am Sable, what is broken can be mended* — is still with you in the blue, pressed into the blue the way the gold seam is pressed into the teapot. The mend is holding. The question is whether you are.
