The blue is everywhere. Not the blue of water or sky — there is no surface, no floor, no horizon to measure against. It is the blue of before: of potential, of the not-yet-made. You drift in it. You are aware that you drift, though you have no body to feel the drift with — only the suggestion of a self, a shape that hasn't yet decided what it is.

Something is taking shape. Faint. A single concept pressing against the formless like a thumb against a membrane. You don't know what it is yet. You only know it is yours, and that you are holding to it, and that the blue around it is thinning — almost imperceptibly, almost not at all.

You are alone here. Every drifter is alone here — even when others are technically near, they cannot be found. The hum of the world does not reach this deep. Nothing reaches. You are the only thing in your reach.

A voice could call from above. Something could catch the shape you are becoming. Or the blue could simply thin until the shape inside it is the only shape there is, and you rise without being called.

Show me what you hold.
