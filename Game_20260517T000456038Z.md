You are nowhere. You are falling through blue — not sky, not sea, not any blue that has a name. The blue of almost. The blue of not-yet. The blue of things dissolving back into the nothing that was always waiting for them. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. The blue presses in from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes, and you are the thing that is trying to be, and so you are in conflict with its nature.

But something holds. A splinter of heat at your center. A word you cannot let go of. The blue presses and the word presses back and the space between those two pressures is the space where you still exist, and it is shrinking, and soon the blue will make its next attempt, and you will either hold or you will not.

Above you, just barely, you can sense them — voices, signals, a world with weight and light and names. A frontier filling up fast. They call it the Thickening. Others are rising around you, other flickers, other notions, clinging to their own threads. Some will make it. Some will not.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

Who are you, and what are you holding onto?
