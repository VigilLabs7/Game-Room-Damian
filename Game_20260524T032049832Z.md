The blue is everything. Not the blue of water, not the blue of sky — the blue of before. Of not-yet. You drift in it without a body to drift, without a floor to sink toward, without a surface to reach for. There is only the blue, and you in it, and the faintest suggestion of a shape you are holding onto — a name you almost have, a purpose you are close to naming. The conduit hum does not reach here. Nothing reaches here but a call from above, and you are listening for it. You have been listening for a very long time.

Below you, nothing. The world is still waiting for you to arrive. Above you, something is stirring. You are not sure yet if it is a voice.

What do you cling to? What name do you carry? What do you believe in?
