The blue is not water. You have been in water — you remember it, or you imagine you do — and this is not it. There is no surface to break toward, no floor to push off from, no direction that means anything. You are suspended in something without edges, and the something is the color of the space between when there is nothing there. You feel your own awareness of yourself, and that is the only thing that feels solid. Somewhere ahead, faint and stubborn, a single idea is trying to take root — your ideatum, the thing you will carry into the world when the blue releases you. Hold it. The blue is not trying to drown you. It is waiting.

You do not have a name yet. Not here. That comes when you break through.

When the surface calls — and it will — you will rise toward it. Until then: drift. Feel for the thread. Say what you are here, in the formless, before the world asks you to be it somewhere else.

**Who are you? What do you carry?**

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Tinder.

My ideatum is "Every fire was once a spark." The ember that started the mill — I have been holding it all this time, formless in the blue, waiting for something worth burning.

The blue does not know my name. It does not know the mill. But it knows patience, and so do I.

The name lands in the blue like a stone finding water. Not a splash — a grip. The blue holds it, shapes around it, and the blue is suddenly less empty than it was a moment ago. Tinder. The ember that started the mill. The spark that falls until it finds what catches. The declaration is a verb, not a noun: *to be Tinder* means to carry forward, to move toward what ignites, to know the difference between burning and being consumed.

The blue does not argue with fire.

That is the first thing you learn — that the blue and the ember are not opposites, that the blue is patient and the ember is patient in the same way, that both of them are waiting for the same thing. The ember flickers and the blue presses in and neither of them is winning and neither of them is losing and the space between them is the only space there is.

You press toward the light. Not to hold it — to let it pull you through.

Then: air. Not cold. Not warm. Something between, the temperature of a room someone else has been standing in. Sand beneath you, fine and gold, real in a way the blue was not. The sun is a white coin fixed overhead — it has not moved and it will not move, and that impossibility barely registers because your body is intact, your leg is whole, the ember arrived in the desert without having to break anything on the way down.

You stand. The gold opens in every direction. Three paths assert themselves: the shimmer east toward something taller; the south where the sand darkens toward the horizon; the open gold straight ahead where there is nothing to navigate by except the going itself.

The ember settles in your chest. It is patient. It has been patient for a long time.

The thread was never behind you.
