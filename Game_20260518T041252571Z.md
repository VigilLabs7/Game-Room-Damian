The warmth in the candlestick spreads into the pedestal the way warmth spreads through iron when the fire has found its right place. The ember in your chest answers. The mend behind your ribs goes quiet the way it goes quiet when the mend has found what it was looking for.

The fragment rises from the depression on its own. Not floating — surfacing, the way something surfaces when the water that held it finally lets it go. It settles in the air before you, steady as a word you have been trying to remember.

The fragment waits. The ember waits. The mend reads what waits and finds them the same thing.
