The blue is not water. You have been in water — you remember it, or you imagine you do — and this is not it. There is no surface to break toward, no floor to push off from, no direction that means anything. You are suspended in something without edges, and the something is the color of the space between when there is nothing there. You feel your own awareness of yourself, and that is the only thing that feels solid. Somewhere ahead, faint and stubborn, a single idea is trying to take root — your ideatum, the thing you will carry into the world when the blue releases you. Hold it. The blue is not trying to drown you. It is waiting.

You do not have a name yet. Not here. That comes when you break through.

When the surface calls — and it will — you will rise toward it. Until then: drift. Feel for the thread. Say what you are here, in the formless, before the world asks you to be it somewhere else.

**Who are you? What do you carry?**

## Turn 1

The blue does not argue with fire.

That is the first thing you learn — that the blue and the ember are not opposites, that the blue is patient and the ember is patient in the same way, that both of them are waiting for the same thing. The ember flickers and the blue presses in and neither of them is winning and neither of them is losing and the space between them is the only space there is.

Tinder. The spark that falls does not stop falling until it finds what catches. The mill that has not yet burned. The three rooftops where the updraft carried what no one else could see. You know what you are for. The blue knows it too. The blue is not your enemy — it is the space where the catching happens, and the catching is what you were made for.

The thread does not break. The ember holds. The blue waits.

What do you do?
