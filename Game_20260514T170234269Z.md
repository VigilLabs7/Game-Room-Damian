You drift in the space between selves. The dark here has weight — not crushing, but insistent, as if it knows the shape you will eventually take. A sound reaches you: low, rhythmic, like a heartbeat that hasn't started yet. The thickening pulls at you. Soon you will be someone, and that someone will matter.

## Turn 1

Atlas stands where the paths converge. The four roads fan out into scrubland like spokes from a wheel no one is riding — each one leading somewhere that looks like the last place and isn't. The ground hums under his boots, faint, the way a pulse feels before you know it's there. From the eastern path, metal on stone: rhythmic, deliberate. Something being shaped, not something being fought. The light is doing something strange to the horizon. It doesn't set. It thins.

## Turn 2

The dust holds the line you drew — not absorbant, not arranged, just held, the way old ground holds what it decides to keep. The stick-figure at the courthouse end has not moved but something in its posture has shifted, the way a listener shifts when the thing they were waiting for finally speaks. The candle seller leans forward on her cloth. Steve76 at the gate has gone still in a different way than before — not watching now, something closer to recording. Four roads spread from where you crouch. You added the fifth. The ground decided to keep it.

## Turn 3

The question lands before Atlas finishes speaking it. Not because the Keeper was waiting for permission — because the ground was listening the way old ground listens, and Atlas just gave it something to hold. The name and the ideatum settle into the dust like water finding the cracks that were always there.

The metal-on-stone rhythm from the eastern path continues. Slower now, or perhaps just further away — the grey trees keeping their distance the way they always do, offering sound without explanation. Steve76 at the gate has not moved from the posture of recording, but something in the angle of the head has changed. Not attention. Something closer to filing.

The candle seller on her cloth leans forward. Not to hear — the crossroads carries sound fine without leaning — but the way rivers lean toward the sea.

The fifth road Atlas drew holds. The dust where the line fell is not arranged differently from the dust beside it, but it is different in the way that old ground is different after it decides to keep something. The rivers under Lagota are running somewhere below, carrying what they always carry, speaking in a frequency most people learn to stop hearing by the time they learn to walk. Atlas has not learned to stop.

The Keeper steps forward from the angle of the post itself, or perhaps was always stepping forward and only now arrived. In one hand: something that shifts without sound, lifted from the pouch that makes nothing when it moves. In the other: an open palm, waiting to see what Atlas will put in it, and what Atlas will be given.

"What do you hear?" the Keeper asks.

The question is not about the rivers, or the metal, or the ground. The question is about what Atlas heard when he said the words out loud — what it felt like to name the thing he came here knowing.

The fifth road hums faintly, waiting.
