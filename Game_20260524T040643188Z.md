The blue has you.

Not in the way water holds a swimmer — there is no swimming here, no body to swim with. The blue is the medium you are made of right now, and you are the blue with a question forming in it. Somewhere between what you were before and what you are about to become. The threshold does not look like anything. It feels like the moment before a word you have not yet said.

You are holding something. You do not know what it is yet. But it is yours and it is the only solid thing in all this depth — a single idea, still taking shape, the only handhold in the formless. The blue knows what you are carrying. It has been reading it while you were still figuring out how to hold it.

Something outside the blue is calling. You cannot see it. You cannot hear it the way the world hears. But the thing you are holding — it answers to the call. It lifts toward the thin place in the blue where the light is different.

You are not here by accident. You were sent. You were chosen. You were noticed, and what noticed you wants you to arrive.

What are you carrying? What name do you hold? What do you believe is true about the world?
