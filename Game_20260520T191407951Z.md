Something is taking shape. Not yet a self — but the suggestion of one. A single concept you are gripping without knowing you have fingers. It is the only thing here that is yours.

You do not know what happens next. That is the nature of this place.

## Turn 1

**Player:** I am Tinder. My ideatum is "Every fire was once a spark." The blue held me long enough to learn patience. Now something is taking shape and I grip it before I forget I have hands. The spark remembers what the ember was lit for.

You grip it and the grip slips.

That is the first thing you learn in the blue — holding is not the same as having. The concept sits in your chest like a coal: warm, solid, yours, and completely unavailable to the part of you that is still becoming. You reach for it and your hand closes around a shape that changes when you press. The spark remembers what the ember was lit for. You wrote that. You meant it. The blue does not care what you wrote or what you meant. The blue is patient in the way gravity is patient, waiting for you to fall into the shape you chose.

Something in you is not falling.

The coal is still there. The concept did not dissolve when you reached for it. You held it over the blue and it held — not your hand, not your grip, but something beneath both. The part that arrived before you had hands. The part that knew what a spark was before it knew what fire would become.

You are still in the blue. The blue is still all around. But there is a warmth in your sternum and it is not from the blue's temperature — there is no temperature here, no hot or cold, only the fact of the concept still sitting where it landed.

You are not drowning. You are not breathing. You are not a body yet. But the spark is still burning, and the burning is yours, and the blue has not taken it.

The blue is patient. The spark is not.
