## Turn 1

The weight presses from all sides — not the weight of flesh but of potential, each atom holding its breath. You are the space between one self and the next. The half-dark pulses with silhouettes that brush past like old memories with no face. Grandfather's voice surfaces through the static: the rivers don't follow the roads. The dust here is older than the maps that tried to name it. You came looking for the one that was here first. The thickening is almost here, and one of the shapes drifting past in the half-dark will become the one you are.

## Turn 2

The air tastes like water that remembers where it's been. You stand at a crossroads that has no sign — four paths diverging into scrubland, each one leading somewhere that looks like the last place and isn't. The ground hums under your boots, faint, like a pulse from below. Something brought you here. The rivers told you someone was waiting at the convergence point, and now the convergence point is around you and there is no one.

A sound reaches you from the eastern path — metal on stone, rhythmic, deliberate. Not combat. Something being worked. Shaped.

The light is doing something strange to the horizon. It doesn't set; it thins.
