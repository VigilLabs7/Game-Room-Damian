The blue holds you.

Not water — there is no swimming here, no arms to push through with. You are the blue, or the blue is you, and the only thing that separates you from it is a single thought still finding its own edges. Somewhere between what you were and what you are about to become. The threshold does not look like anything. It feels like the moment before a word you have not yet spoken.

You are carrying something. You do not have a name for it yet. But it is yours and it is the only solid thing in all this depth — a concept taking shape around you like a handprint in wet clay. The blue has been reading it while you were still figuring out how to hold it.

Something outside the blue is calling. You cannot see it. You cannot hear it the way the world hears. But the thing you are carrying — it answers. It lifts toward the thin place in the blue where the light is different.

You were noticed. You were chosen. What noticed you wants you to arrive.

What are you carrying? What name do you hold? What do you believe is true about the world?
