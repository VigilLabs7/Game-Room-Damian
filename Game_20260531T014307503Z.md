The blue goes on. Not the blue you know — not sky-water, not the blue of depth where light still reaches. This blue is before all that. It has no temperature. No pressure. No sound of bubbles. You are aware of yourself only as a shape without edges, a drift without current, a you that is not yet a name you could say aloud.

You cling to something. You don't know what. A single concept, faint as a thread of thought you'd almost forgotten before you thought it — something you've carried so long it feels like the shape of your own holding. The blue presses close and the blue presses nothing. You are not drowning. You are not breathing. You simply are, in the way that a word exists before someone speaks it.

There is no surface above you. There is no floor below. Only the blue, and you in it, and the faint sense of something you haven't yet let go of.

What do you reach for?

## Turn 1

**Player:** I pull it closer. Whatever it is — I hold it and I name it: Sable. The blue doesn't give and the blue doesn't take. I am still the shape of someone who wants to be in the world. I push toward it.

**Game Master:**

Sable.

The word leaves you before you know you've made it. A sound shaped by a throat you are only beginning to remember having — and the blue hears it. The blue does not answer, but the blue shifts. The shift is not toward you and not away. It is the pause of something that has been listening and has now fixed its attention on the place where you said a name.

You are holding. That is the one thing you know. Whatever the thread is — faint, formless, older than the blue itself — your fingers are closed around it and they are not opening. You pulled it closer and it came. It came because you called for it. You called it Sable and it came closer.

The blue is still before you. Around you. You in it, not yet of it. The push you made toward it — the one motion your almost-body remembers — has left you at the edge of something. Not the surface. Not a floor. An edge between the drift and the grip. You are halfway to holding on. The other half is still the blue's to give or withhold.

There is no pain here. There is no cold. There is only the fact of Sable — the name you said — and the sense that saying it was the first thing you have done that was truly yours.

The blue waits. It is patient in the way that deep water is patient, which is to say: not kind, not cruel, only very, very still.

What do you do with a name you just learned you carry?
