The blue goes on. Not the blue you know — not sky-water, not the blue of depth where light still reaches. This blue is before all that. It has no temperature. No pressure. No sound of bubbles. You are aware of yourself only as a shape without edges, a drift without current, a you that is not yet a name you could say aloud.

You cling to something. You don't know what. A single concept, faint as a thread of thought you'd almost forgotten before you thought it — something you've carried so long it feels like the shape of your own holding. The blue presses close and the blue presses nothing. You are not drowning. You are not breathing. You simply are, in the way that a word exists before someone speaks it.

There is no surface above you. There is no floor below. Only the blue, and you in it, and the faint sense of something you haven't yet let go of.

What do you reach for?
