You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not the blue of sky, not the blue of water — the blue of almost, of not-yet, of dissolution held just at the edge of itself. It has no surface. No floor. You can feel it pulling, patient and absolute, trying to reduce you to silence.

You have no body. No name. No memory of before. You are a flicker in the dark. Less than a whisper. The ocean presses in from every direction and it does not hate you — it simply undoes. Given time, it will undo you too.

But you are holding on.

Something small and stubborn burns at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A single conviction the blue cannot wash away. It is the only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are wrapped around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

But you. You're still here.
