The blue has no memory. It holds you the way water holds a body that has forgotten how to swim — not gently, not harshly, just completely. You are the only thing here that knows how to be something. Somewhere above, a voice is reaching for the thing you carry. The thing you carry knows it too — it hums faintly at the edge of hearing, the shape of a truth you almost have a name for.

You rise toward the sound. The blue thins around your edges like a membrane giving way.

**What do you do?** Speak your name, or offer what you carry — or let the threshold choose for you.

**Game Master:**

The question reaches the blue and finds nothing to catch it. The blue is not unkind — it is simply prior to kindness, prior to everything. You asked what you hold and the answer you offered was someone else's words: a teacher in a temple, a verse about emptiness, the observation that meaninglessness stands at the center of things. But the teacher was not you. The verse was not yours. The question you were asked was simpler and harder: not what do others believe, but what do *you* hold?

The voice from the blue asked again, and your answer was another question. That is not an ideatum. That is the space where an ideatum might grow, if you planted one — but you did not plant. You drifted. The blue thins in the direction of nothing in particular. You are still here. You have not taken hold. The drift continues.

The voice is patient. It will ask once more.
