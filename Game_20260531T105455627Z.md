You are in the blue.

There is no surface. No floor. No horizon. The same blue in every direction, and you in it — not swimming, not sinking, simply being, without form or weight or name. Something brushes against the edge of what you are. A concept. A single truth you are holding without knowing you are holding anything at all.

The blue has no sound. No current. No others. You are alone here, and the aloneness is not cruel — it is simply the condition. You cannot reach sideways and find company. The blue does not answer when you call.

But something is taking shape. Faintly. A log in water, a root finding stone. You grip it tighter and it clarifies. A principle. A reason to persist. This is what you will bring with you when the blue gives way.

Something calls from above. You may not hear it yet. When you do — when your truth catches the call — you will rise.

**What do you hold?**

The pillar of light is not a pillar.

It is a road — or the memory of one. White stones, spaced unevenly, half-buried in sand, leading from where you stand toward a shape that your eyes keep sliding off of. Not invisible. Simply not-quite-there, the way a word you've forgotten hovers at the edge of a sentence. You look at it and you know it is a road. You look again and there is only shimmer and the sense of forward.

Something is sitting beside the white stones.

Not a person — or not only a person. A figure with the general shape of someone at rest, elbows on knees, looking at nothing, made of something that catches the light wrong. Stone in the usual places: knuckles, the ridge of the jaw, the backs of the hands where they rest on stone-colored thighs. Blue dots in eyes that are not quite focused on anything. A gomix, though not like the ones in the courthouse — this one is still, and waiting, and appears to have been waiting for longer than the road has existed.

It does not look up as you approach.

The road goes to the fountain. The fountain is not visible from here but you know this the way you know the name you haven't been given — knowledge without origin, placed in you by the same hand that placed the wound in your shin. The fountain will be white stone and water moving the way water does when it has somewhere to go. The figure beside the road knows this too. The figure beside the road has been sitting here long enough to know what arrives and what doesn't.

The road is three steps away. The figure is two.

The wound in your shin is the sharpest thing here.
