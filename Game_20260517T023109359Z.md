The blue finds you before anything else does. No sound. No sensation except the pull — a slow, patient drag from every direction at once, as if the ocean is breathing you in and has no intention of stopping. You were something once. You think. The edges of that memory have gone soft, worn down by the pressure of the deep.

You have a thread. One. It burns faintly in the center of what you are — a phrase, a weight, something you cannot set down and cannot forget. The blue hates it. You can feel that hatred in the way the water pushes against the heat, trying to cool it, trying to quiet it, trying to make it just another part of the drift. But the thread holds. It is the only thing that does.
