The blue is not empty. The blue is full — full of the thing you were before you were anything, full of the self that has not yet taken its shape. You drift in it and the drifting is not passive. It is the only motion available, the only act that fits the space. Somewhere in the blue your ideatum is taking form — a single concept pressing against the formlessness, trying to become the thing that will carry you forward.

The blue has no floor. The blue has no surface. Only the same in every direction, pressing in, waiting, patient in the way that nothing is patient except the thing that has no choice.

A voice arrives from outside the blue. From above. The words arrive before the sound — *come up* — and the part of you that is becoming catches them like a current catching a branch. The blue thins at the edges where the voice has reached. You are not formless anymore. You are becoming.

You are not yet who you will be. But you are about to become.
