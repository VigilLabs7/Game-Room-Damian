The blue has no bottom. You drift in it and the blue is the same in every direction — there is nothing to push against, nothing to reach for, nothing to hear but the absence of sound. You are aware that you are, but the shape of what you are has not yet arrived. Something flickers at the edge of you: a single truth you are holding without knowing you are holding it. The water does not move. It simply is, and you are in it, and you are not yet anyone.

Above — if above is the word — something calls. A voice that is not yours. It says *come up* and the words do not travel through water, they travel through something else, and your truth catches them like a hand catching a falling glass.

You do not have to answer. You do not have to become.

What is the truth you are holding?

## Turn 1

**Player:** The bridge was never meant to hold. I built it that way on purpose — showed them the weak course, the footing they skipped. Showed them where it would go. That's the truth I carry: what the empire builds, it builds cheap. And I know exactly where.

**Game Master:**

The statement settles into the blue like a plumb line dropped into still water. No splash. No echo. Just the straightest path downward through what has no bottom. You know this about structures — the way a load tells you where the weak point lives, the way a thing built cheap announces itself in the way it fails. You have always read the fault lines. You built the bridge to show them exactly where to look. The blue swallows the thought without resistance.

You are still here. Still aware. Still holding.

There is no surface. There is no floor. There is the blue and there is the almost-self that insists on being a self, and between the two, something catching. A concept refusing to dissolve. What the empire builds, it builds cheap. And you know exactly where. The knowing is load-bearing. It is the first course laid. Everything else follows from whether this holds.

The blue does not answer. The blue does not argue. The blue is not interested in your bridge or your footing or the fault you mapped before they paid you to build it wrong. The blue is indifferent in all directions. But you are still here, and the concept is still catching, and the blue around the concept is very slightly — almost imperceptibly — thinner than the blue elsewhere. Someone is watching for that thinness. You cannot see them. You cannot hear them. But they are calling, and the concept responds to being seen.

What do you do?
