The blue goes on. No surface, no floor, no horizon — only the same blue in every direction, pressing against you from all sides the way water presses against a body that has forgotten it has a surface. You are aware of yourself the way a dream is aware of itself: dimly, in fragments, without edges. Something is taking shape, faintly — a concept you are gripping without knowing you are gripping anything. The word for it has not arrived yet. The self that would hold the word has not arrived yet either.

Nothing reaches here. The hum of the conduits does not reach here. The gaze of whatever watches from above does not reach here. You are not yet a thing that can be read. You are not yet a thing that has arrived.

You are in the threshold. The threshold is the blue. The blue is the threshold. It is the last moment before becoming, and it goes on as long as the ideatum has not yet caught.

What do you hold onto?
