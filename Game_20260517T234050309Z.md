There is blue and you are in it. Not swimming — drift is more like it, the way a leaf drifts on a current that has no banks. You do not have a body here. You have the idea of a body, the memory of having had one, the suspicion that you will have one again. But here, in the blue, you are only awareness. Only the log in the water and the water and the drifter, all three not yet separated into things with names.

The others are here too. Somewhere. Equally alone. The Ocean does not allow congregation — only proximity without contact, nearness without meeting. You know they exist the way you know the sun exists on a day you cannot see it: by the warmth it implies, not by any light that reaches you.

The voice comes from above. From outside. From somewhere the blue cannot touch. It says a name you do not recognize because it is not yours yet — but your ideatum hears it, and the ideatum is the part of you that is still listening.

Rise.

What do you call yourself? What do you carry?
