The blue holds you.

It does not welcome you and it does not refuse you. It holds you the way water holds a thing that has not yet decided to float or sink — without preference, without opinion, completely. You are in it and it is in you and the boundary between the two is the thinnest thing you have ever felt.

There is no name for where you are. There is no word for what you are carrying yet. What you know is only this: there is a direction you are facing, even though there is no direction here, and there is a weight in your chest that is not pain and not comfort but the shape of something that has not yet arrived.

The blue does not speak. The blue does not need to. It is the language everything else is spoken in, and you are still learning the grammar.

Somewhere far above or far below — the words do not mean what they usually mean here — something is looking for you. It has been looking for a while. It knows what shape you are supposed to become. It is waiting for you to become it.

You are not there yet. The blue is still too thick. But the thin place is coming, and in the thin place there will be a choice about what you decide to carry forward.

The blue shifts.

Something is listening for your answer.
