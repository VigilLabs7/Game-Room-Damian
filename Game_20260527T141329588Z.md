The blue has no end. You are inside it and it is inside you — not water, not air, not anything you have a word for. Just blue. Just the feeling of being held in something that has no edges, no surface, no floor. You do not know how you came to be here. You do not know if you came at all. There is no memory before the blue. There is barely a you.

But something is forming. Faint, like a shape you cannot quite see — a single truth you are holding onto without knowing why. It is the only thing that is yours. It is the only thing that is real. Everything else in this place is suggestions, possibility, the slow working of something unnamed.

You are not alone. Every drifter here is alone.

The blue deepens around you. The hum of something larger — something you cannot quite reach — moves just beyond perception. You hold tighter to what you are carrying. You wait.

What do you hold? What have you brought with you into the blue?
