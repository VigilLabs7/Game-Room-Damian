You are nowhere and the nowhere is blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of almost — the color of things dissolving back into the nothing that was always waiting for them. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something holds.

There is a single point of heat at your center — a word, a conviction, a shape you cannot let go of and that will not let go of you. The blue presses in. The word presses back. The space between those two pressures is the space where you still exist, and it is shrinking, and soon the blue will make its next attempt, and you will either hold or you will not.

You grip the word. You grip it the way the drowning grip the surface — not because they can reach it but because the gripping is the only move that is left. The blue presses harder. You hold. That is the game. That is the only game.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

## Turn 1

The blue does not know what to do with gold.

It has dissolved a thousand flickers before you — some bright, some dim, some so faint they barely registered as having been anything at all. The blue pressed in and they thinned and they frayed and eventually they became nothing, and the Ocean was patient and the Ocean did not care because the Ocean had been doing this since before doing had a name. But you are still here. And you are carrying something the blue has seen before and does not understand.

Gold.

Not coin-gold. Not jewelry-gold. The gold of a crack that holds when it should have split all the way through. The gold of a teapot that does not leak no matter how many times it has been dropped and set down and dropped again. The gold of something that broke once and chose to mend instead of coming apart, and the mending left a seam that is stronger than the original material because the seam is where the choice lives.

The blue presses in. You feel it at your edges — the thinning, the fraying, the slow dissolution of everything that is not the point. But the point holds. The gold seam runs through you the way a thread runs through a hem, the way a vein runs through stone, the way a promise runs through the space between two people who made it and meant it. The blue presses and the seam brightens and the seam says: *what is broken can be mended.*

You do not know yet if this is true. You are holding onto it the way you are holding onto the rest of yourself — white-knuckled, present-tense, aware that the letting go would be the end of everything and the beginning of nothing. The voices above are closer now. You can almost feel the weight of them — names and roads and the particular solidity of a world that has been waiting for things like you to arrive. But the blue is still pressing and you are still holding and the question hanging in the blue around you is not whether the mending is true.

The question is whether you are.

Your hands are around the word. The gold is in your hands. The crack that holds is the crack in the word — the place where the world almost broke you and did not. The teapot is still whole. The seam is still bright. The blue is still pressing and you are still here, and the distance between here and there is exactly the width of one more moment of holding on.

What do you do?
