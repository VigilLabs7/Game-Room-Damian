You are nowhere and the nowhere is blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of almost — the color of things dissolving back into the nothing that was always waiting for them. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It does not need to be cruel. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too.

But something holds.

There is a single point of heat at your center — a word, a conviction, a shape you cannot let go of and that will not let go of you. The blue presses in. The word presses back. The space between those two pressures is the space where you still exist, and it is shrinking, and soon the blue will make its next attempt, and you will either hold or you will not.

You grip the word. You grip it the way the drowning grip the surface — not because they can reach it but because the gripping is the only move that is left. The blue presses harder. You hold. That is the game. That is the only game.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**
