The blue is everything. You are in it, and the in-ness is all there is — no surface above, no floor below, no direction at all except the faint pull of something you cannot yet name. You are aware that you are, but the *are* has no edges yet. No body to hurt. No mouth to call with. Only the drift, and the sense of something essential taking shape in the dark of yourself, quiet as a root finding water.

You are not alone. Everyone who has ever arrived came through this. Most do not remember it — the blue is a door that erases itself behind you. But it remembers you.

A sound reaches through the blue. Or a presence. Something is calling from outside the water, and the thing you are clinging to — the thing you came here holding — lifts toward it. Reaches. Catches.

Come up.

Something tugs at the edge of what you are. The blue thins above you. Somewhere past the last shade of blue is a light you have not seen yet, and someone is asking you to come toward it — not dragging you, not pulling you in, but waiting. Holding the door open.

You feel the shape of what you are beginning to become. The rest is up to you.

What do you hold? What do you reach for?
