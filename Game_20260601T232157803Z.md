The blue has no bottom. You drift in it and the blue is all there is — above, below, beside. No light reaches here that you could call a sun. No sound arrives that you could call a voice. You are aware of yourself the way you might be aware of a shape in fog: present, but not yet solid.

Something is taking shape in you. Not a memory — you don't have those yet. Not a name. What you have is a single thought, small and bright, catching in the nothing like a finger on a thread. You grip it without knowing you are gripping anything.

Then: a voice. From somewhere outside the blue. Calling.

Come up, it says. Come up, darling.

Something in you answers. You don't know what it is yet. But it answers.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen.

The harbor is gone. The fountain is gone. The twelve stones in your pocket — gone. The figure turning toward you with something to say — gone. Your hands. Your body. Your name in someone else's mouth. You said the words and the words did not catch.

You hang in the black, weightless, unanchored. There is nothing to hold — no stone, no shard, no voice from the blue. The black is thick and it goes all ways. Somewhere behind you there was a harbor and a fountain and a life you were ready to resume. You reached for it. The reaching is already gone.

There is nothing to do, because there is nothing to do it in.

What do you do?

You stop reaching. The outline is gone. And in the going of the outline, something else arrives — not the harbor, not the fountain, not the twelve stones or the figure deciding whether to speak. Something less. Something more.

There is a shape where absence used to be.

You push harder and your fingers find a seam — vertical, cool, not stone but close to it. A surface. An edge. Something that is, when everything else has been unmade.

Somewhere on the other side of this surface is whatever the Angel carried you toward. The shape where absence was. The seam in the nothing. The surface that answers when you push.
