The blue is not water. You have been in water — you remember it, or you imagine you do — and this is not it. There is no surface to break toward, no floor to push off from, no direction that means anything. You are suspended in something without edges, and the something is the color of the space between when there is nothing there. You feel your own awareness of yourself, and that is the only thing that feels solid. Somewhere ahead, faint and stubborn, a single idea is trying to take root — your ideatum, the thing you will carry into the world when the blue releases you. Hold it. You do not have a name yet. Not here. That comes when you break through.

When the surface calls — and it will — you will rise toward it. Until then: drift. Feel for the thread. Say what you are here, in the formless, before the world asks you to be it somewhere else.
