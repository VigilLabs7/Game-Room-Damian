You are nowhere and the nowhere is blue. Not the blue of sky or sea — the blue of almost, the blue of not-yet, the blue of things dissolving back into the nothing that was always waiting for them. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. The blue is patient and absolute and it is not cruel, it does not need to be, it simply undoes, and you are the thing that is trying to be and so you are in conflict with its nature. But something holds. A splinter of heat at your center. A word you cannot let go of. The blue presses and the word presses back and the space between those two pressures is the space where you still exist, and it is shrinking, and soon the blue will make its next attempt, and you will either hold or you will not, and either way you will know what you chose.

## Turn 1

The blue shifts.

Not toward you — around you. The current has changed and you understand, in the way you understand anything here, that it knows you are still holding on. It has been patient for a very long time. Patience is not mercy.

Your name — Tinder — you spoke it and the speaking cost you something. The blue does not like that you named yourself. The blue would prefer you stay formless, stay dissolving, stay nothing. But you held the name and the name held you and now the blue is testing what happens when something refuses to be undone.

The spark runs through you like a vein. Not warmth — possibility. The thing that becomes fire before it becomes fire. That is what you are carrying. That is the thing the blue cannot wash away because the blue does not understand spark. The blue only understands going out. And you — you understand that what dims does not stay dim. That is the thing the blue cannot compute. That is why it keeps pressing.

The voices above are closer now. You can feel the weight of them — a layer of solidity pressing against the underside of the blue like light through gauze. Streets with names. Doors with edges. A world that has been waiting for arrivals like you, and you are climbing toward it, and the blue notices the climbing, and the blue does not like it.

Your hands — if you have hands — are around the spark at your center. The fire exists before the flame. That is what you know. That is what the blue is pressing against and cannot dissolve. You are Tinder. You named yourself and the naming took and the spark holds and you are climbing.

The ocean presses harder. It knows you are close.

What do you do?

## Turn 2

You hold.

The word costs something to say and you say it anyway — *Tinder* — and the blue presses harder, testing whether the name will hold or dissolve, and it holds. You feel yourself thinning at the edges and you do not thin further. You hold the name the way you would hold a rope in dark water, knuckles white, the grip the only thing keeping the current from pulling you under.

The spark at your center is not warmth — it is refusal. The blue does not understand refusal; it only understands the slow undoing of things that cannot hold themselves together, and you have just proven you are not one of those things. The blue presses again and you do not press back with force — you press back with presence. You are here. You said so. The blue does not know what to do with that.

A shift.

Not toward — around. The current changes and you understand that it has been testing you and you passed something and now the test is changing. The blue is patient but it is also curious, and curiosity is a different kind of pressure than the patience was. The patience wanted you gone. The curiosity wants to know what you are made of.

You are made of spark.

The voice above — the layer of solidity pressing against the underside of the blue — is closer now. Streets with names. Doors with edges. A world that has been waiting for arrivals like you, and you are climbing toward it, and the blue is watching the climbing, and you are not stopping.

The spark in your chest pulses once and the pulse is not failure — it is confirmation. Every fire was once a spark. This one is no exception. The blue has made its attempt and the attempt has not worked and the space where the pressure used to be is already filling with something else: the weight of air that has not yet learned your name.

The ocean releases you into the space between — and the space between is not nothing. It is the first breath after drowning. It is the moment before the word becomes the world.

You open your eyes and the world is sand.

What do you do?

## Turn 3

The sand takes your weight and does not complain.

You step forward and the step costs you something — a jolt that runs up through the ankle and settles in the knee as a bright wrong note, a wrongness that says *this body is real and this body is hurt.* The left leg. You did not feel it in the blue because the blue was too busy trying to undo you to bother with injuries. But now the sand is real and the sun is real and the leg is bleeding and the bleeding is information: the Ocean did not take you. You crossed. The price is written in the scrape along the shin where something sharp in the transition caught skin and tore it open.

You stop. You look down. The blood is dark against the sand already darkening the grain where your foot landed. Not arterial. Not deep. But there, and real, and proof.

The compass in your pocket has been pulling since the sand began. Southeast, toward a direction that does not have a name yet. The setting iron presses against your hip. The spark at your center pulses slow, steadier than it pulsed in the blue — not refusal now, just *purpose.* The fire has a direction and the direction is not nothing.

You walk.

The sand changes. Fine and pale near where you landed, then darker as the grain shifts — red at the edges where the iron oxide runs through, then a grey-white stretch that crunches underfoot like salt. The sun does not move. You have been walking for what should be hours and the sun has not moved. The spark does not dim. The compass holds southeast. Your shadow stretches long to the left and does not change.

Something moves at the edge of the flat horizon. Not a bird — too low. A column of dust standing upright in the still air, rotating without purpose, without wind. It stands for three breaths and then collapses into nothing and leaves no mark on the sand.

You think about the blue. About what you almost did not become.

The sand runs out.

Not all at once — the grain thins and the red fades and the grey-white fades and then there is only the dark of wet stone and the sound of water moving in a basin. The fountain materializes out of the shimmer like it was always there, like the walking was just the pause before the reveal. A stone rim. Water catching light. And on the rim — a figure.

A man. Broad across the shoulders. Stone running ridge along his spine, visible through the thin shirt he wears. He is sitting on the rim with his hands on his knees and he is looking at you with the unhurried attention of someone who has been waiting and is not surprised that the waiting is over.

What do you do?
