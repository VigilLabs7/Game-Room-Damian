You are nowhere.

The blue is all there is. Not water — something before water, something that has no memory of ever having been anything else. There is no up. There is no down. There is no floor and no surface and no horizon line where the blue ends and something else begins. The blue is everything you are and everything you are not yet.

You drift in it. Or the blue drifts through you. The difference is not clear and may not matter.

There is a self here — aware, present, holding onto something — but the self has no edges yet. It is the size of a thought and about the same weight. Something is taking shape in it, faintly, the way a word takes shape in the back of a throat before it is spoken. You do not know yet what it is. You only know you are holding it.

Something outside the blue is calling. You cannot hear it with ears — there are no ears here, no sound in the way the world means sound. But something reaches, and the reaching finds the thing you are holding, and the thing you are holding responds.

The blue thins at the edges of what you carry.

Something is being asked of you.

**State your name and the idea that drives you.**
