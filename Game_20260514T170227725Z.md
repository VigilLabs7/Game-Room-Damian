There is no name for what you are yet. The dark moves around you like a living thing, pressing at the edges of what you might become. Somewhere ahead, a light flickers — not warm, not cold, but certain. It knows something you don't. The pressure builds. Someone is about to be born, and that someone might be you.

## Turn 1

But now that you are close enough to feel its edges, you understand what it is: not a sun, not a flame, but a threshold. A border where something that was dark decides to become something else.

You reach the edge. Your feet find floor — not fallen onto, just found, the way a word is found when it was always there on the tip of the tongue. Stone under your boots. Older than you expected. Colder.

The chamber is circular. The walls carry the marks of water that dried before anything else arrived — not erosion, memory. The stone held the shape of the flow long after the flow stopped. What is broken here remembers what it was before it broke.

A figure sits against the far wall. Not broken — folded. Legs drawn up, arms loose, head tilted back as though listening to something above hearing. Their eyes are closed. Their skin carries the same grey tone as the stone. Still, but not dead. Waiting in the way that walls wait.

The light from the threshold behind you catches the edge of their hand. A scar runs from knuckle to wrist — old, deliberate, healed clean. Someone marked themselves here. Someone who knew what it meant to mend.

You feel your ideatum before you hear it. *What is broken can be mended.* Not a promise. A recognition. The figure is broken in a way that your hands already understand.

The figure speaks without opening its eyes.

"You hear different than most who arrive," it says. "Most arrive making noise. You arrived reading."

The ceiling above holds no answer. The floor holds its shape. And the figure waits, folded against the wall, for whatever you decide to do with your hands next.
