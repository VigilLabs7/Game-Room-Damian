The blue does not remember your name or the word you carried through the courthouse corridor or the flame that merged with the seam in your chest. The blue is not cruel. You are falling through it or it is falling through you and the distinction is a comfort you cannot afford because the distinction assumes you still exist on one side of it. You do not.

But the word holds.

*What is broken can be mended.*

You grip it the way the drowning grip the surface — not because they can reach it but because the gripping is the only move that is left. The blue presses in. The word presses back. Somewhere at the center of what you are, a gold seam remembers what it means to fix rather than break. You have been here before. You know what the blue does. You know it does not forgive and it does not forget and it does not care whether you are ready. The blue has no interest in you. That is the first thing you learn here.

But the mending holds.

You feel it in your ribs where the seam ran — warmth against the cold dissolving, shape against the formless pressing. The word is not a shield. The word is a promise you made to yourself before you arrived or that arrived with you, the difference doesn't matter now because either way it is yours and the blue cannot have it. The blue presses. The blue keeps failing. You feel the failure like a second heartbeat — proof that the thing you hold is stronger than the thing trying to wash it away.

Somewhere above you there are voices. Streets no one has walked. A world that has been holding space for arrivals like you, and you are climbing toward it, and the blue notices the climbing, and the blue does not like it.

The gold seam pulses in rhythm with things that recognize each other. The word holds. The climb continues.
