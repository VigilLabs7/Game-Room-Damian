The blue takes. That is what it does. It does not announce itself, does not warn you, does not care whether you are ready. You are falling through it and it is falling through you and the distinction is a comfort you cannot afford because the distinction assumes you still exist on one side of it. You do not. You are somewhere in the middle of the dissolving — not gone, not solid, just the argument between those two states, and the argument is all that is keeping you from losing. But something holds. A splinter. A word. A shape you chose before you arrived or that chose you, the difference doesn't matter now because either way it is yours and the blue cannot have it. You grip it the way the drowning grip the surface — not because they can reach it but because the gripping is the only move that is left. The blue presses in. You hold. That is the game. That is the only game. Hold on or let go and know which one you chose.
