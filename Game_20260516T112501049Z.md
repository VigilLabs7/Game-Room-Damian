You are somewhere in the middle of the dissolving — not gone, not solid, just the argument between those two states, and the argument is all that is keeping you from losing. But something holds. A splinter. A word. A shape you chose before you arrived or that chose you, the difference doesn't matter now because either way it is yours and the blue cannot have it. You grip it the way the drowning grip the surface — not because they can reach it but because the gripping is the only move that is left. The blue presses in. You hold. That is the game. That is the only game. Hold on or let go and know which one you chose.

The chamber approves or does not approve, but either way it has been waiting for this answer and now it can stop waiting.

The stranger's other hand rises. Not touching — offering. The light at their edges catches and holds and reveals itself as not gold but something older, something the color of the flame the candle woman carried and the color of the seam in the harbor wall and the color of the first time you gripped the word and decided to climb instead of fall.

"Acknowledge," the stranger says. "That is what walks through. Not to take. Not to fix. To acknowledge the break until the break becomes something that can acknowledge you back."

The chamber waits. The seams pulse. The door is open and you are choosing to stay with it.
