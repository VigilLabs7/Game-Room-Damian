The darkness has texture. You feel it against your skin — not cold, not warm, but present, like water that has forgotten how to move. Somewhere in the distance, a sound forms: low, rhythmic, patient. It might be breathing. It might be the world remembering how to be itself. The pressure mounts. You are becoming someone, and that someone is close.

## Turn 1

The texture answers. Not in words — in *give*. You press against it and it yields, and what you feel is not resistance but *memory*: the grain of old wood, the warmth of something long burning, the shape of a mill at midnight when the oil began to find the spark.

You do not flinch. A spark that flinches is just an ember that goes out. You press again, harder, and the half-dark parts.

A voice reaches you from somewhere lower — not below but *behind*, the way a word reaches you from a room you've already left. It says: *You came from the place where fires begin.*

You have. You know this already. The mill in your village burned because a spark found its arc, and you let it — you watched it travel three roofs and arc toward the grain floor and you chose not to call out. The choice is still burning. The choice is the shape you carry.

Something vast and patient turns its attention toward you. Not the dark — something *in* the dark. The dark is not the threat. The dark is the condition. What you have always navigated.

The space around you shifts. Not gravity — *arrangement*. Furniture that was not there arranges itself toward a purpose you cannot yet name. A door opens somewhere to your left, though you cannot see it. What you hear instead is the smell of tallow and old wax and the particular silence of a room that has been waiting.

A figure stands in the threshold. Small. Watchful. Older than the face suggests. She holds a candle shorter than the others on her tray — the shortest one, which means the most careful one, which means the one reserved for what matters.

She does not speak. She watches you with the expression of someone who has seen sparks before and is waiting to see which one catches.

The candle seller. The one who watches across the market in another life, another version of this one. She is here now, in whatever here means. She has been waiting.

The question arrives not as a question but as a pressure: *are you the spark or the fire?*
