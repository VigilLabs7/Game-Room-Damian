The blue has no interest in you. That is the first thing you learn here — the blue is not cruel, not hungry, not out to get you. It simply is, and its nature is to undo, and you are the thing that is trying to be, and so you are in conflict with the fundamental character of this place. You are falling through it or it is falling through you — the distinction stopped mattering some time ago, because time is a thing that happens in a world with clocks and you cannot find a clock in the blue, only the endless slow dissolve of everything that thought it was something.
