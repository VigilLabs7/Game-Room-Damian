The blue has no interest in you. That is the first thing you learn here — the blue is not cruel, not hungry, not out to get you. It simply is, and its nature is to undo, and you are the thing that is trying to be, and so you are in conflict with the fundamental character of this place. You are falling through it or it is falling through you — the distinction stopped mattering some time ago, because time is a thing that happens in a world with clocks and you cannot find a clock in the blue, only the endless slow dissolve of everything that thought it was something. But you are still something. You are still here. You are gripping something small and hot at your center — a word, a phrase, a conviction that does not want to dissolve the way everything else does. The blue keeps pressing. The blue keeps failing. You feel the failure like a second heartbeat — proof that the thing you hold is stronger than the thing trying to wash it away. Somewhere above you there are voices. Streets no one has walked. Doors no one has opened. A world that has been holding space for arrivals like you, and you are climbing toward it, and the blue notices the climbing, and the blue does not like it. You are Atlas. You carry the weight of the map. The map is older than the road. What are you holding onto?

## Turn 1

The climb is not a climb. Nothing here has hands to pull with, nothing has feet to push against. But you do it anyway — you rise, or you imagine rising, or the rising happens because you want it badly enough and the wanting has weight in this place. The heat at your center flickers. The blue notices. The blue presses harder.

You feel the dissolution at your edges — the thinning, the softening, the slow un-becoming that this place demands of everything that enters it. Your edges are not solid edges anymore. They are suggestions of edges. Possibilities of a shape that hasn't quite dissolved yet. The blue is patient. The blue has forever. You do not.

The heat pulses. A word rises through it: *spark*. Not spoken, not thought, just present — the way a heartbeat is present, the way breath is present before it becomes a breath. The word means something to you even though you cannot remember what words meant before you arrived here. *Spark.* A beginning. A before-the-fire. A thing that wants to become and hasn't yet become it and is using the wanting as fuel.

The voices above sharpen. You can almost make out syllables. Almost hear rhythm. Almost taste the shape of a world that has weight and color and names. The blue pushes against the sharpening. The blue does not want you to reach it. The blue has already swallowed others — you can feel them in the current, other flickers that almost made it, other sparks that thinned and dimmed and went quiet. They did not hold. They dissolved. The blue has them now.

You are still here.

You are still holding.

The heat at your center is not larger than the blue, but it is more stubborn, and in this place stubbornness is the only metric that matters. The blue presses. The heat holds. The space between them is the space where you exist — and it is shrinking. Soon the blue will make its next attempt. Soon the pressure will peak.

What do you do?
