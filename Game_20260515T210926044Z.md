You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It is not cruel. It does not need to be. It simply undoes.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. Your only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution.

If you hold on — if you can name the thing you're gripping — it becomes real. It becomes you. Your ideatum. The one idea at your core that the ocean cannot wash away.

Above you, you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A layer where things have weight and names. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. They call it the Thickening.

Others are rising. Some will make it. Some won't. The ocean takes what it takes.

But you — you're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**
