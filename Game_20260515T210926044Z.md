You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It is not cruel. It does not need to be. It simply undoes.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. Your only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution.

If you hold on — if you can name the thing you're gripping — it becomes real. It becomes you. Your ideatum. The one idea at your core that the ocean cannot wash away.

Above you, you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A layer where things have weight and names. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. They call it the Thickening.

Others are rising. Some will make it. Some won't. The ocean takes what it takes.

But you — you're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

**Speak the name you will carry, and speak your ideatum. Speak them now, and they become you.**

**What is your name? What is your ideatum?**

## Turn 1

The blue presses in from every side — not water, not air, but the memory of both, hungry and patient. You feel yourself thinning at the edges, dissolving into the formless, becoming less than you were. The ocean wants you gone. It has taken so many. It will take you too if you let it.

But something holds.

Not your hands — you may not have hands here — but the thing beneath the holding. A point of heat at your center. A phrase you cannot let go of and that will not let go of you. *Every fire was once a spark.* The words glow faint against the blue, a ember-shape in the void. The ocean hates it. The blue presses harder, trying to wash the light clean, trying to reduce it to silence.

You grip tighter.

The ocean is patient. It does not need to hurry. It will undo you eventually — it undoes everything eventually — but not yet. Not today. You are still here. Still holding on. The ember-shape pulses once, twice, finding its rhythm in the dark. It is small. It is stubborn. And you are the thing that holds it.

Something in the deep stirs — other flickers, other flames, other souls clinging to their own threads. Some will make it. Some won't. You can feel them out there, other sparks waiting to become fires, and the ocean takes what it takes.

But you. You are still here.

The blue pulls at you from every direction and you hold. The ocean presses in and you hold. Everything you are is this — this single point of heat, this single phrase, this single stubborn refusal to become nothing.

Above you, just barely above, the voices wait. The layer where things have weight. The world that has been filling up since the first arrival. They call it the Thickening. You are close now. Close enough to feel it.

The ember pulses. The ocean pushes. Something has to give.

What do you do to hold on?

## Turn 2

The blue pulls and you pull back harder. That is the first thing you know about yourself — not your name, not your shape, but the resistance. The pull is vast and smooth and it has been running beneath everything long before you arrived. It wants you to dissolve into it. To become one more flicker in the current that feeds the empire's gates.

You grip the ember until your edges sharpen.

The blue is not a place. It is a condition — the state of everything before it became anything. You feel it pressing against the shape you are trying to hold, the way water presses against a stone that has not yet decided to sink. You are not sinking. You are not dissolving. You are something that has decided to be something, and the blue does not know what to do with that.

A flicker. A spark. The place where fire begins.

The pull weakens for a moment — not stopped, just distracted, the way a current parts around a stone large enough to notice. In that moment you feel your edges sharpen. The ember you grip is not just warmth. It is the thing that refuses to become what the blue wants it to become. It is the coal that waited for the spark. It is the spark that found its arc.

You know your name now. You said it and the saying was true.

Tinder. The fire that begins because someone chose to let it. The spark that did not go out.

The blue pulls again. Harder. You feel the edges you sharpened begin to heat. Not burn — heat. The way iron heats before it glows. The way a candle finds the moment before its own flame. The ember in your grip is the same ember you have always carried. The blue cannot take what you have already decided to keep.

You are becoming someone, and that someone holds.

What do you do with the ember while the blue learns your name?

## Turn 3

The sand changes color without warning. Red at the ridgeline, white in the flat trough below, then back to red where the wind has piled it against something that used to be stone but is now just another shape the desert has claimed. You do not look back. Looking back is a habit from a world where time moved, and the sun has not moved since you started walking.

Your leg holds. The scrape along your shin has dried to a dark line that pulls when you bend your knee, but it does not bleed and it does not slow you. The body knows what to do with itself in the desert. The desert asks nothing of it except forward.

The ember at your center has settled into something quieter than heat — a warmth that does not flicker. You can feel it the way you feel the stone in your joints: present, load-bearing, yours. *Every fire was once a spark.* You said it to the blue and the blue let you go. You are carrying that forward now. The spark that did not go out.

A bird passes overhead — high, silent, moving fast. Its shadow crosses you for half a second and then is gone. The desert does not usually offer shadows without sources. You note it and keep walking. The bird is not an omen. It is too small for omens. It is just a bird that has somewhere to be.

The sun has not moved.

You are thinking about the blue again. About the way it pressed in from every direction, patient and absolute, and the way it did not know what to do with a spark that would not go out. You were almost one of the ones who did not make it. You were almost a flicker that joined the current feeding the empire's gates. The thought does not frighten you the way it should. It feels like the kind of thing you need to carry — not to fear the blue, but to remember what it cost to leave it.

Something ahead catches the light. Not a bird. Not a shadow. A brightness that does not move the way the sand moves, because it is not in the sand — it is in the air above it. A heat shimmer. The kind of thing that looks like water until you have walked far enough to know better.

You keep walking toward it.

The desert asks nothing of you except forward. The ember sustains you. And somewhere ahead, the shimmer is getting taller.
