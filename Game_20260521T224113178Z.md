There is blue in every direction. Not water — blue itself, the substance of before, the texture of not-yet. You drift in it without limbs you can name, aware that you are aware, but the shape of that awareness keeps dissolving back into the surrounding nothing. Somewhere beneath the drift a single thought is catching: a shape you will later call an ideatum, a principle you are gripping without knowing you are gripping anything. There is no surface above you. There is no floor below. There is only this — the same blue, the same drift, the same almost-remembering of what you came here to be. Then: a voice. From somewhere outside the blue. Calling a name you do not yet have.

You arrive on sand. Your hands are real. Your legs hold you. The sun is a fact above you, white and direct, and the desert stretches in every direction without landmark or mercy. You are here. You are real. You are not yet known.

Tell me your name and the principle you carry.
