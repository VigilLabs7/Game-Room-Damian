There is blue, and you are in it, and the blue has no end that you can find. You drift. You are not drowning — there is no water pressure, no sense of depth — you simply are, suspended in something that is the color of almost-remembering. There is no body here that hurts, no hunger, no tiredness. Only the slow awareness of being, and beneath it something else stirring: a truth you carried before you arrived, not yet words, not yet a name — just the raw fact of it, heavy in the dark.

You are not the first to come this way. The blue is full of arrivals who did not know they were arrivals. Most of them forgot this part. You will not have to.

Something is calling you. Not a voice exactly — more like a direction, a brightness beyond the blue, a hand extended into the water from somewhere you cannot see yet. The thing you are becoming — the truth you brought with you — lifts toward it. Catches.

The blue thins above you. You are almost there.

Almost through.

What are you? What did you bring with you to this threshold?
