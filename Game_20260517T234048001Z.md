The blue has no floor. No surface. No horizon. You drift — or you are drifted — through a color that has no name, in a direction that has no meaning. There is only the sense of holding something. A single idea, not yet words. A log in the water and you are the log and the water and the drifter all at once, before those words meant anything.

You are not alone here. You are not together. The others are somewhere in the same blue, equally apart, equally unable to reach — the Ocean does not permit company. Only the fact of other drifers, somewhere, the way loneliness exists even when you cannot see the faces.

Then: a voice. From above. Somewhere outside the blue entirely. Calling a name you do not yet have, toward a place you cannot yet imagine.

Your ideatum stirs. Whatever you are here, whatever the idea is that you cling to — it catches the call. It responds. You feel yourself rising.

What do you call yourself? What do you carry?
