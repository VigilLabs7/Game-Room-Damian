The blue is everything. Above, below, beside — the same soft weight in every direction. No light reaches here that you could call sun. No sound arrives that you could call voice. You are aware of yourself the way you might be aware of a shape in fog: present, but not yet solid.

Something is taking shape in you. Not a memory — you don't have those yet. Not a name. What you have is a single thought, small and bright, catching in the nothing like a finger on a thread. You grip it without knowing you are gripping anything.

Then: a voice. From somewhere outside the blue. Calling.

Come up, it says. Come up, darling.

Something in you answers. You don't know what it is yet. But it answers.

The blue has no bottom. You drift in it and the blue is all there is.

Something brushes the edge of your awareness. A shape that is not yet a shape — the faintest suggestion of a concept hovering just beyond your reach, like a word you knew once and have forgotten. You cannot read it. You can only grip it, if you can grip anything at all. The ideatum has not yet taken. The blue does not care. It holds you the same way it holds everything: indifferently, completely, without promise.

What are you?
