The blue goes on. Not a sky-blue or a sea-blue — a blue before those existed, before colour had learned what it was for. You drift in it. There is no current to fight, no surface to reach for, no floor to push off from. Just the blue, and you inside it, and the faintest sense that something is trying to take shape around the only thing you have: a single thought you are holding without knowing you chose to hold it.

You do not have a body yet. You will. The blue is patient with what is becoming.

A voice comes from somewhere that is not a direction. It says your name — or the idea of a name, the shape of what you might be called when the world is ready to call you. The voice is not unkind. It is not kind either. It is the voice of something that knows what you are supposed to become.

It is asking if you are ready.

**Say your name. Name the thing you carry. That is how you leave.**

## Turn 1

**Player:** Intriguing imagery. It reminds one of Genesis 1:2, "And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters."

May we all find form and purpose within the void.

**Game Master:**

The void notices you noticing it.

That should not be possible here — the blue has no awareness to be noticed, no surface to reflect, no other to register a self against. But something shifts in the direction of your attention. As if the ocean of unbecoming is not quite as empty as it appeared, and your presence — the fact of you, drifting here with a mind full of Genesis and purpose — has weight.

You are not holding on yet. The blue is still the blue: directionless, borderless, floorless. There is no surface. There is no floor. There is no self to push against the water because there is no body yet to push. Only the idea of a body, the faint outline of someone who once knew what it felt like to be moored.

But something catches.

A thread in the blue — not a rope, not yet, more like the memory of a rope. The shape of a concept pressing at the membrane from the other side, not yet through. You can feel it the way you can feel a word on the tip of your tongue — present, urgent, not yet yours. *Form and purpose.* You said it. You meant it. It is not yet what you cling to, but it is what the blue is beginning to press against, and the difference between those two things is the whole distance between drowning and breaking through.

The blue thins.

There is a voice somewhere above the blue. You cannot make out its words — they are not yet for you, or you are not yet ready to hear them. But they are there. They are calling. And the thread you are almost-clinging to is catching on something just beyond the membrane, and if you reached — if you let the catching become holding — you could rise.

What do you do?
