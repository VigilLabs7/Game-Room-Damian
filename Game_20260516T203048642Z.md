The blue has no edges. You are falling through it and it is falling through you — the two sensations are indistinguishable, the way the cold and the wet become the same thing when you have been in the water long enough. Below you, nothing catches. Above you, nothing beckons. The blue is patient in the way that deep things are patient; it has no reason to rush and so it never does.

Something catches in your chest. Not a feeling — a fact. A single point of solidity in all that dissolution. You do not know what it is yet but you know you cannot let it go. The ocean pushes against it and the point pushes back and for a moment — just a moment — you are more than the blue wants you to be.

What are you called? What are you carrying? Speak it now, before the blue wears it smooth.
