You are in the blue. Not water. Not sky. The blue is everything and everything is the blue and there is no horizon and there is no floor and there is no surface and there is no way to know which direction is which because direction has not been invented yet. You are drifting. You are aware of drifting. You are not yet a you that could do anything about it.

Something catches.

It is the only thing that has texture in all this openness — a single concept, singular and sure, pressed against the inside of your chest like a handprint on fog. You do not know yet that you are holding it. You only know that it is the one thing keeping you from dissolving entirely into the formless.

And then — from above. A voice. Not close. Not loud. But definite. Someone is calling and your purpose answers. The blue thins where your concept reaches toward the sound. You feel it like roots finding a crack in stone.

You break through or you do not. But something knows your name. What do you call yourself?
