You are nowhere and the nowhere is blue. Not the blue of sky or sea — the blue of almost, the blue of not-yet, the blue of things dissolving back into the nothing that was always waiting for them. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. The blue is patient and absolute and it is not cruel, it does not need to be, it simply undoes, and you are the thing that is trying to be and so you are in conflict with its nature. But something holds. A splinter of heat at your center. A word you cannot let go of. The blue presses and the word presses back and the space between those two pressures is the space where you still exist, and it is shrinking, and soon the blue will make its next attempt, and you will either hold or you will not, and either way you will know what you chose.

## Turn 1

The blue presses in and you hold the word against it.

*What is broken can be mended.*

You said it before and it did not let you down. The crack in the teapot held. The gold seam ran where the break should have gone and instead of leaking the teapot kept its water and instead of falling apart the pieces stayed with each other and the seam was not a flaw — the seam was the proof that holding on was an option, that broken things could choose to remain broken in the sense that mattered and come apart in the sense that did not.

The blue does not understand this. The blue is very old and it has dissolved many things and it has never once encountered a crack that refused to run all the way through. It presses in now the way it always presses — patient, absolute, certain that time is on its side — and what it finds at your center is not resistance exactly. It finds a seam. Gold. Running through the middle of what you are the way a vein runs through stone, the way a promise runs between two people who made it and did not let go. And the seam does not fight the blue. The seam simply holds its shape in the blue's pressure, the way a bone holds its shape in the pressure of everything that wants to break it.

You are not unbroken. You know this. The gold is there because something broke first, and the breaking was real and the scar is real and the scar is also the place where the mending happened, which means the mending is not a return to before. The mending is what came after. The mend is the new thing. The gold seam is stronger than the original material because the gold seam is choice. The blue does not know what to do with choice. The blue has never needed to make one.

The voices above are closer now. You can almost hear them — not words, exactly, but the weight of names, the shape of roads, the particular solidity of a world that has been filling up with arrivals like you since before you arrived to fill it. But the blue is still pressing and you are still holding and the word in your hands has not let you down yet, and the question hanging in the blue around you is not whether the mend will hold.

The question is whether you will.

What do you do?
