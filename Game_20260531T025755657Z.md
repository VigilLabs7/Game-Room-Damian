The blue goes everywhere. Not water — blue. The color of something that hasn't decided what it is yet. You are in it, though you don't have a body exactly, not yet, just the suggestion of one — the memory of arms, the idea of legs, the faint sense that you are shaped roughly like a person and not like anything else. There is nothing to push against and nothing to pull toward. You drift. Somewhere above you — not a direction exactly, more like a meaning — something is calling. Not to you. Not yet. It's calling to what you might become. Your ideatum is here with you, though you don't have a word for it yet. It's the thing you cling to. The thing that will pull you up when the call comes. You don't have a name. You don't have a shape. But you have this: a single truth, still formless, waiting to be born.

What do you do?
