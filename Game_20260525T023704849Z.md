The blue has no edges. You are inside it and it is inside you and there is no point at which one becomes the other — only the slow sense of something you are carrying, something that was handed to you or maybe left inside you before you knew you had it, a single conviction that has not yet learned how to speak.

You drift. There is no current and no current is needed — the blue carries you by being everything, by leaving nothing out. You have no body yet that has weight. You have no name yet that fits. What you have is a direction, a tilt, the way a compass tilts toward north not because it chooses to but because it cannot do otherwise.

A voice. From somewhere outside the blue, reaching in. You do not recognise the voice but you recognise the intention — something is summoning, something is calling, something has noticed you specifically and wants you to come.

The thing you carry responds before you decide whether to let it. It lifts. You feel yourself rising toward the thin place in the blue where the world is waiting.

You are not lost. You are on your way.

What are you carrying? What name do you hold? What do you know to be true that you have never been told?
