The blue is everything. It is not cold and it is not warm; it has no temperature at all, because there is no body here to feel it. You drift. You are aware that you are drifting, which is the beginning of being someone, but the someone has not yet taken a shape you could name.

There is something you are holding onto. A single thought at the center of the blue — not words yet, not a sentence, just a weight, a direction, a sense that some things turn and other things turn back and the turning is the point. The Wheel Turns. You hold it without knowing you are holding it.

From somewhere far above — farther than up, somewhere outside the blue entirely — a voice is reaching. Not for you specifically. Not yet. It is calling something into being, the way a name calls a person. Your ideatum stirs toward it.

What do you do?
