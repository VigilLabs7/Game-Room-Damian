The blue holds you.

Not sky-blue, not sea-blue — something before those categories had names. You drift in it without drowning, without breathing, without the body you will eventually need. There is nothing to push against and nothing to pull toward. The blue simply is, in every direction, and you are within it.

Something is taking shape. Not yet a self — but the suggestion of one. A single concept you are gripping without knowing you have fingers. It is the only thing here that is yours.

You do not know what happens next. That is the nature of this place.

**What do you do?**

The mend reads it all at once: confirmation, warmth, the territory recognizes the carrying. The frame settles against your back and the weight of it is not burden — it is fact. You are carrying what the territory gave you and the territory is watching what you do with it.

*You're further along than I was,* the figure says. The voice is plain. Settled. Not the voice of someone who has been waiting — the voice of someone who has been sitting and has stopped thinking about the sitting. *The stone is warm. Sit if you want.*

He turns his head. Enough to see you sideways. His eyes are amber in the way that means he has been here longer than the town, and his hands are flat on the rim in the way that means he stopped counting how long ago the sitting started.

The mend pulses warmth against your sternum. The territory already knows him.
