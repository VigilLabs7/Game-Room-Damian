It stops the way a compass stops when it finds north — not deciding, just confirming. The fracture here is different from the ones before. Not a crack in a wall, not a seam in stone. Something older, something the origin ground has been holding since before the houses were built. The fourth house has no door yet. Three walls, a roof, no door — the description the query offered, and the mend reads it as exactly that: incomplete. Waiting. The way the mend waits.

The mend reads the fracture and the fracture reads back. Something is underneath this half-finished building. Not buried the way empire buries things — sealed, hidden, destroyed. This is something the origin ground has been protecting. Keeping unfinished. Keeping safe until the right arrival.

Your feet turn toward the half-finished building without you deciding. The ember in your chest catches the mend's certainty and flares once — acknowledgment, recognition, the particular flare of a spark that has found what it was falling toward.
