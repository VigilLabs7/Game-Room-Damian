You are in the blue. Not blue as in water — blue as in before-color. Before-light. Before the world decided what it was. You are aware of yourself as a shape that has not yet taken its shape, and around you the blue goes on in every direction the same. There is no up to rise toward and no down to sink into. There is only the drift, and the faintest sense of something you are holding — something you brought with you from wherever you were before you were here.

Somewhere far above, or far within, or nowhere at all, a voice is calling. You cannot make out the words yet. But the voice is saying your name. Not the name you have — the name you will. The one that will fit when you finally know what you are.

You are not drowning. You are not suspended. You are becoming.

What do you hold?

You hold tighter. The rope thickens. The self that is not yet a self presses harder against the inside of what it is becoming.

Something shifts.

It is not a sound. It is not a light. It is a thinning — a place where the blue goes slightly, fractionally, almost imperceptibly lighter. As if the blue itself is not uniform. As if there are places in it where the self that you are has worn through to something underneath, or beyond, or before. You do not move toward them. You do not need to. The holding does it. The act of gripping what is yours draws the world open in the places where you are most yourself.

A voice from nowhere, from everywhere, from the place where the rope is thickest: not yet. Not yet. Not until you know what you hold.

You are not nothing. You are holding something. The blue thins.
