You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. It is the Ocean of Unbecoming — where things that almost were dissolve back into things that never will be.

You have no body. No name. No memory of before. You are a flicker. A tremor. Less than a whisper. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It is not cruel. It does not need to be. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. Becoming less. Un-becoming.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at the center of what you are. A notion. A phrase. A conviction you cannot let go of — or that will not let go of you. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. The blue presses in, tries to wash it clean, tries to reduce it to silence. But it holds.

You are clinging to it now. It is a log in the void. A raft. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here. If you let go, you are nothing. You were never anything. The ocean wins.

But if you hold on — if you can name the thing you're gripping — it becomes real. It becomes *you*. Your **ideatum**. Your core. The single idea that will carry you out of the blue and into a world that is waiting for you to arrive.

Above you — just barely above you — you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A layer of existence where things have weight and light and names. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. A frontier filling up fast. They call it the Thickening.

Others are rising. You can feel them in the deep — other flickers, other notions, clinging to their own logs, fighting the same current. Some will make it. Some won't. The ocean takes what it takes.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

**Speak the name you will carry, and speak your ideatum. Your name is the word others will call you in the world above. Your ideatum is the one idea, the one phrase, the one conviction at your core that the ocean cannot wash away. Speak them now, and they become you. Your name your shape. Your ideatum your nature, your weapon, your shelter. They are the only things you own and the only things you are.**

**What is your name? What is your ideatum?**
