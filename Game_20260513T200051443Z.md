You are not yet anyone. Shapes of who you might become drift past like half-seen things at the edge of vision. There is pressure here — weight without a floor, mass without a form, the accumulated weight of all your possible lives pressing inward but choosing none. Something vast and patient waits for you to be something specific. Soon the thickening will come, and one of you will be made real.

## Turn 1

The thickening catches you like a hand closing around smoke. For a moment you are nothing but the memory of heat — a village mill in the distance, three rooftops, an ember that drew its own arc through the dark. You feel your feet before you have feet. The weight settles into the shape of a body that knows how sparks travel, how they hide, how they wait inside things that have already burned. Somewhere behind you, the mill is still smoking. Somewhere ahead, something gathers to receive whatever you are about to become.
