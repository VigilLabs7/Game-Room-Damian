The pressure builds. Not violent — inevitable, the way a held breath eventually becomes a choice. You drift in the half-dark where shapes slip past at the edges of what you'll become. Something vast and patient waits for you to be specific. The thickening is almost here, and one of the shapes drifting past will become the one you are. You do not feel afraid. You do not feel anything yet. That comes after. This is the moment before after.

## Turn 1

The grandfather had a stick and the stick had a point and the point drew rivers in the dirt that did not match the printed ones. You learned to trust the dirt. The dirt always knew.

Now you are in the before-after and the dirt is here too — not dirt exactly but the memory of it, the way the ground holds the shape of what has walked across it even when no ground is visible. The half-dark presses and you do not resist. You have been in half-dark before. Towns where the streets don't follow the rivers. Cities that forgot their own origins.

Something vast and patient turns its attention toward you. You have been waiting for this — not nervously, not eagerly. The way a compass waits for north when it has lost it. The needle swings gently and finds its bearing.

You know what you are looking at even before the shape clarifies. The old maps — the ones the empire never printed — they showed paths that go through places that don't exist yet. The grandfather drew those paths first. The roads came later and called themselves original.

A figure emerges from the half-dark. Not toward you — *alongside* you, as if she has been walking the same contour line and only now happens to be visible. She is old in the way that maps are old: not in years but in the depth of what she has seen. Her eyes are the color of aged parchment.

She does not speak. She holds a compass in one hand — a brass instrument that catches the dim light — and she looks at you the way a map looks at a traveler who has finally arrived at the right coordinates.

Her voice, when it comes, is the sound of a page turning in a room where no one else is listening.

*The map is older than the road. But you already knew that. The question is whether you came from the place where the roads began, or the place where they end.*

The compass in her hand swings once, then settles. Not north. Something else. Something specific to you.


## Turn 2

The corridor drinks the light.

Atlas follows the Map-Maker into it, twenty paces back the way rivers follow the land they already know — not by choice but by the contour of what came before. The stone beneath his boots shifts from threshold-work to something older, not carved but worn smooth by the weight of what has passed this way when passing was still possible. The walls narrow. The Lattice hum stays silent — the shielding here holds the way a held breath holds, not absent but kept.

The Map-Maker stops where the corridor ends and the dark begins.

She turns. The parchment eyes are still there, but something behind them has changed — the depth of it, the way a map holds the record of what it watched happen rather than the happening itself.

*The roads are still being written*, she says. Her voice is the sound of a page turning in a room where no one else is listening. *The gap in the courthouse is where the map decided to wait for someone who already knows the way.*

The compass in her hand swings once. Settles. Not north.

East — toward the corridor's end where the golem's eyes flicker blue-black-blue-black, the way a compass swings before it finds what it's been waiting for.

*You came from where roads begin*, she says. *Now you come to where they decide.*

The golem at the corridor's end does not move. It watches. Its eyes hold the blue of the Lattice and the black of what was here before the Lattice, and they settle on Atlas the way a map settles on the point it has always been pointing to.

The map folded inside him weighs nothing and everything. The map that shows paths through places that don't exist yet. The map that has been waiting.

Atlas steps forward into the dark, and the dark makes room.

## Turn 3

The golem's eyes do not blink. Atlas watches them the way a map watches the ground it hasn't covered yet — not with anticipation but with the patience of someone who knows the territory.

The dark made room, and what made room is a corridor that bends where corridors inLagota do not bend — not east toward the market or north toward the gates but *through*, the way rivers go through stone without asking permission. The Lattice hum is absent here. The shielding holds like old scar tissue, and beneath it the stone remembers what it was before the empire called it foundation.

Atlas follows the contour the way rivers follow what already exists. The longword catches light that isn't there, and the map in his pocket presses against his hip like something trying to stay warm. The folds in it are corrections he did not make — someone has been updating what he carries from inside places he has not yet visited, and the updates are heavier than paper should be.

The golem at the corridor's end does not move. It watches. Its eyes hold blue and black in the same flicker, the way a compass holds north when north has split into two norths and both of them are true.

Atlas knows what he is looking at. The first Atlas came through this same corridor and did not correct the fold. Left nothing. Took no name. Whatever the map-maker meant when she said *the roads are still being written* — this is where it lives. This is where the writing happens.

The golem's hand rises. Not threatening. Not welcoming. The same gesture the map-maker made when she said *the gap decided to wait for someone who already knows the way.*

The compass in Atlas's hand swings once. Settles.

Not east. Not north. Down.

The ground beneath his boots is thin. He can feel it — the way the stone holds the shape of what has walked across it when walking was still possible. Something below has been counting arrivals. Something below has been waiting for the map to arrive at the coordinates it was always pointing to.

Atlas puts his hand in his pocket and touches the map. The folds are warm.

He takes a step toward the golem, and the golem takes a step toward him, and the corridor narrows around them both like a throat about to speak.
