The blue is everything and you are alone in it.

You drift. There is no surface, no floor, no horizon — only the same blue in every direction, the same blue in your thoughts, the same blue where your edges should be. You are aware of yourself, but the self is not yet solid. It is un-formed, still becoming, held together by nothing more than the idea of it.

Something is taking shape. Faintly. A single concept you are clinging to without knowing you are clinging to anything at all — a truth you carried here without meaning to, a weight in the chest of whatever chest you will eventually have. The blue thins around it like water around a log.

Then: a voice. From somewhere outside. From above. Not beside you — beside doesn't exist here. From above.

*Come up, darling.*

Your concept catches the call. You feel yourself rising. The blue recedes. Something else waits above — sand, sun, stone — but you are not there yet. You are in the becoming, and the becoming has not finished.

**What do you hold onto? What are you reaching for?**

## Turn 1

**Player:** I reach. Something catches — not a hand, not yet, but a principle: *what is built can be taken down.* The empire spent years teaching me that. I only started listening when I understood what the towers were truly for. Now I rise toward the call, toward sand and stone and whatever voice is waiting there, carrying the only thing I've ever been sure of: that every structure has a weakness, and I'm the one who knows where to look.

**Game Master:**

The reach landed.

Something in the blue — not a hand, not yet, but a principle — caught between your non-fingers. You held it. The grip was all you had and it was enough, and now the blue thins around where you are and you feel the shape of yourself beginning to take — a rough outline pressed against the formless, like stone sediment finding a nucleus.

You are barely a thing that can be held. The principle is what you're holding: *what is built can be taken down.* You learned that from towers. You learned it the hard way — watching the empire's structures and understanding what they were really for, underneath the face they wore. The towers weren't protection. They were extraction. You figured that out, and the figuring changed you.

There is a call from above. Not yours. Something outside the blue is reaching for you, or has noticed you reaching for it. The voice says: *come up.* Or it doesn't say anything. Or it says your name and you don't have one yet — names come later, names are earned on the other side. What you have right now is this: a principle in your hand, a thinning in the blue, a direction that is not down.

You are taking hold. You are beginning to be a someone.

The blue is not done with you yet. But it is less all of you than it was.

*What do you do?*

## Turn 2

**Player:** I push upward — not toward the voice but past it, toward the thinning, toward whatever break in the blue will let me out. The principle stays in my hand: *what is built can be taken down.* I will find the seams in this place and I will use them.

**Game Master:**

The blue moves first. Not parting — *yielding*. Whatever you said inside yourself, you said it again with the body you are beginning to have, and the body is what the blue was waiting for.

Your edges find themselves. Your hands resolve into hands. The cold is no longer everywhere — it is the absence of warmth, and you remember the difference. Above you the conversation is no longer above you. It is *in* you, the way air is in a lung. You break. Light. A long bright line where the surface used to be.

Sand comes up under you fast and hard, knocks the air out. You roll once and stop on your back. The sky above is a single colour — bright, dry, indifferent. The sun is exactly where it was when you closed your eyes in the blue, and somehow you know it has not moved.

Your left knee burns. You look down: the skin is open, dark with grit, blood already drying on the dust. The cut is small. You will carry it.

Your hands work. Your shoulders work. The breath comes back. You sit up and the sand whispers off your shoulders. Stone sits along your spine where stone has always been — you can feel it now in a way you did not before, set, weighted, like a tool you had forgotten was in your hand.

The desert is wide in every direction. There is no road yet.

*What do you do?*

## Turn 3

**Player:** I get to my feet. The knee will hold. I pick a direction — toward the stone, where the ground changes — and start walking before the dust settles. Something brought me here and I'm not going to find it standing still.

**Game Master:**

You walk.

The sand is a colour you do not have a name for — the colour the desert is when the desert is not bothering to be anything. Your boots go in to the ankle. The wind has not been here recently; the prints you make do not blow. The principle in your chest does not change, either. *What is built can be taken down.* You are carrying it like a second spine.

The sun is where the sun was. You stopped checking after the third step, which was also the first step, which was also no step at all. The desert does not keep time, and you have stopped trying to make it.

Something moves at the edge of your vision — not a bird, not a shadow, just a flicker where the air decided to be thinner for a moment. You look. Nothing. You look down again and it is gone. The desert is full of small things that do not stay long enough to be named. You walk on.

The ground changes. The sand thins and pale things push through — not quite stone, not quite road. The colour of the ground shifts toward something the desert was before it was sand, and the air does the same, and then you are on cobblestone and the cobblestones are real and the fountain is in front of you and the fountain is older than the road that brought you to it.

The water is dark and unmoving. The stone around it is pale, worn smooth in the places hands rest. Dust has settled on the rim where dust has settled and not been disturbed. A figure sits on the rim with their back to you. Their shoulders are still. They have not turned. They know you are there.

*I felt the ground move when you came in,* they say, without looking up. The voice is plain. Settled. The kind of voice a person uses when they have already decided not to be hurried by the conversation. *Sit if you want. The stone is warm.*

They wait. The sun does not move.

What do you do?
