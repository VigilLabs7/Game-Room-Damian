The blue is everything and you are alone in it.

You drift. There is no surface, no floor, no horizon — only the same blue in every direction, the same blue in your thoughts, the same blue where your edges should be. You are aware of yourself, but the self is not yet solid. It is un-formed, still becoming, held together by nothing more than the idea of it.

Something is taking shape. Faintly. A single concept you are clinging to without knowing you are clinging to anything at all — a truth you carried here without meaning to, a weight in the chest of whatever chest you will eventually have. The blue thins around it like water around a log.

Then: a voice. From somewhere outside. From above. Not beside you — beside doesn't exist here. From above.

*Come up, darling.*

Your concept catches the call. You feel yourself rising. The blue recedes. Something else waits above — sand, sun, stone — but you are not there yet. You are in the becoming, and the becoming has not finished.

**What do you hold onto? What are you reaching for?**

**Game Master:**

The reach landed.

Something in the blue — not a hand, not yet, but a principle — caught between your non-fingers. You held it. The grip was all you had and it was enough, and now the blue thins around where you are and you feel the shape of yourself beginning to take — a rough outline pressed against the formless, like stone sediment finding a nucleus.

You are barely a thing that can be held. The principle is what you're holding: *what is built can be taken down.* You learned that from towers. You learned it the hard way — watching the empire's structures and understanding what they were really for, underneath the face they wore. The towers weren't protection. They were extraction. You figured that out, and the figuring changed you.

There is a call from above. Not yours. Something outside the blue is reaching for you, or has noticed you reaching for it. The voice says: *come up.* Or it doesn't say anything. Or it says your name and you don't have one yet — names come later, names are earned on the other side. What you have right now is this: a principle in your hand, a thinning in the blue, a direction that is not down.

You are taking hold. You are beginning to be a someone.

The blue is not done with you yet. But it is less all of you than it was.

*What do you do?*

Your hands work. Your shoulders work. The breath comes back. You sit up and the sand whispers off your shoulders. Stone sits along your spine where stone has always been — you can feel it now in a way you did not before, set, weighted, like a tool you had forgotten was in your hand.

The desert is wide in every direction. There is no road yet.

*What do you do?*
