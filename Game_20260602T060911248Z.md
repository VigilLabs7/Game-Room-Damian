The blue has no edges. You float in it and there is no up, no down, no current to follow — only the soft and even weight of water that is not water, pressing in from every side without force. You know you exist. The rest has not yet arrived. Something at your center is taking shape: a single conviction you are gripping without knowing you are gripping it. The blue goes on. You go on inside it. There is no floor. There is no voice yet except a distant one — *come up* — and your truth snags on it like cloth on a nail.

You do not have to answer. You do not have to become.

What do you hold at the center of yourself?

**Game Master:**

The world goes black.

Not the absence of light. The absence of having-been-seen.

The fountain is gone. The cobblestones are gone. The figure turning toward you is gone. Your hands. Your body. You said the words and the words did not catch.

You hang in the black, weightless, unanchored. There is nothing to do, because there is nothing to do it in.

**What do you do?**

**Game Master:**

Something is missing.

You know it is missing because there is a shape where it used to be. Your fingers said so — they closed on the outline of a space and the space was empty and the emptiness had edges, had weight, had the precise dimensions of something you carried and set down and cannot now pick back up. You said the words to mark yourself. Sable. The black heard the word the way a wall hears a name shouted in a corridor: it carried, but it did not answer.

Your fingers found the shape of the missing thing and could not fill it. That is not nothing. Most things lost in the black are simply gone — no shape, no outline, no memory of where they stood. Yours left a mold. You are still standing in the mold. Whatever left it is not you.

The shape where it was is not empty the way the rest of the black is empty. It is empty the way a grave is empty — with the knowledge of what used to be pressed into the sides. You can feel the contours. Something was here. Something fit this space. The black has not forgotten. It is holding the shape of the missing thing the way your fingers closed on it a moment ago, the way you are closing on your name now, the way the name you said belongs to you whether the world agrees or not.

The black does not agree. The black does not disagree. The black holds the shape and waits.

What do you do?
