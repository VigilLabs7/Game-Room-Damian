The blue is everywhere. Not the blue of water, not the blue of sky — the blue of before. Of not-yet. You drift in it and the drifting is all there is. No floor to push against, no surface to reach for, no air to fill lungs you do not yet have. The blue holds you the way silence holds a room: completely, and without interest.

Something is taking shape. Faint — a warmth, a pressure, a word you are almost saying. You cling to it without knowing you are clinging. It is the only thing that is yours.

A voice comes from somewhere outside the blue. Not beside you. Not below you. From above — a direction you cannot point to but can feel. It says: *Come up.*

Your name is not yet a name. Your body is not yet a body. But the concept in your chest has caught something, and the catching is enough. The blue thins. Something rises.

**Who are you? What do you carry?**

Sand begins to suggest itself at the edges of the blue. Not sand yet — the memory of sand, the shape of what will arrive. The formless is thinning.
