You are in the blue.

There is no surface. No floor. No horizon. The same blue in every direction, and you in it — not swimming, not sinking, simply being, without form or weight or name. Something brushes against the edge of what you are. A concept. A single truth you are holding without knowing you are holding anything at all.

The blue has no sound. No current. No others. You are alone here, and the aloneness is not cruel — it is simply the condition. You cannot reach sideways and find company. The blue does not answer when you call.

But something is taking shape. Faintly. A log in water, a root finding stone. You grip it tighter and it clarifies. A principle. A reason to persist. This is what you will bring with you when the blue gives way.

Something calls from above. You may not hear it yet. When you do — when your truth catches the call — you will rise.

**What do you hold?**

**Game Master:**

You hold.

That is all you do. You hold — and the blue presses in from every side, not drowning, not crushing, just present, just waiting, and you grip what you have and you do not let go. A bridge. A toll-road. A relay-tower. The empire's bones showing through where the cheap stone cracked. The slow fuse. The dry powder. You count down without meaning to and the counting is its own kind of holding, the way you hold your breath when you are waiting for something to either break or hold.

The blue is patient. It has always been patient. It will wait for you to let go or wait for you to be pulled under or wait for you to rise — and the rising is not something you do yet, not quite, you are still in the moment before the rising, still gripping the thing you chose not to drop, still learning what it weighs when nothing else is holding it.

Something shifts. A current that is not a current, a sense that the blue is listening — or that you are finally hearing what the blue has been saying all along. You are not alone here. You have never been alone here. The blue is full of others who held something and did not let go and rose when the time came.

You hold. You count. You wait.

The question is not whether you will rise. The question is what you will bring with you when you do.
