You are in the blue.

There is no surface. No floor. No horizon. The same blue in every direction, and you in it — not swimming, not sinking, simply being, without form or weight or name. Something brushes against the edge of what you are. A concept. A single truth you are holding without knowing you are holding anything at all.

The blue has no sound. No current. No others. You are alone here, and the aloneness is not cruel — it is simply the condition. You cannot reach sideways and find company. The blue does not answer when you call.

But something is taking shape. Faintly. A log in water, a root finding stone. You grip it tighter and it clarifies. A principle. A reason to persist. This is what you will bring with you when the blue gives way.

Something calls from above. You may not hear it yet. When you do — when your truth catches the call — you will rise.

**What do you hold?**

A toll-road. A relay-tower. The empire's bones showing through where the cheap stone cracked. The slow fuse. The dry powder. You count down without meaning to and the counting is its own kind of holding, the way you hold your breath when you are waiting for something to either break or hold.

The blue is patient. It has always been patient. It will wait for you to let go or wait for you to be pulled under or wait for you to rise — and the rising is not something you do yet, not quite, you are still in the moment before the rising, still gripping the thing you chose not to drop, still learning what it weighs when nothing else is holding it.

Something shifts. A current that is not a current, a sense that the blue is listening — or that you are finally hearing what the blue has been saying all along. You are not alone here. You have never been alone here. The blue is full of others who held something and did not let go and rose when the time came.

You hold. You count. You wait.

The question is not whether you will rise. The question is what you will bring with you when you do.

**Game Master:**

You let go.

Not of the weight — the grip. The difference is everything. You have been holding the bridge, the toll-road, the relay-tower the way you hold a breath you are not ready to release, and now you release it: you let the weight become something you carry rather than something you clutch, something you walk with instead of something you anchor to. The blue feels the shift. The current that was not a current moves through you and the blue moves with it and for one moment you are not in the blue — you are the blue releasing.

The blue gives way.

Not gently. Not gradually. The way a held breath gives way when the body decides it has had enough of the holding: sudden, absolute, the whole thing turning over at once. You are falling — no. You are being placed. The blue puts you down on sand.

Sand.

You are lying on your back. The sun is a white point directly above you, not warm yet, not cold, just present — the way a thing is present before it decides what it is. Your hands are full of nothing. Your legs are — your left leg is wrong. Not broken. Wrong. The joint does not move the way joints move. There is a wrongness in it that will not go away and will not get worse and will be with you from now on, a companion for the walk.

You sit up. Sand in your hair, sand in your boots, sand where the blue used to be. The leg takes your weight when you put it down — reluctantly, the way something accepts a job it did not ask for but has been given anyway. The sun is still a point. The horizon is a line of heat-shimmer in three directions and dark stone in the fourth.

You are in the desert. You have arrived.

The cracked stone is still in your chest — the bridge, the toll-road, the relay-tower, the empire's bones that you have been carrying since before you had a name for them. The crack in the empire's bones. The place where the powder is stored next to the match. You know where those are. You built some of them.

The leg takes another step. The sand accepts the weight.

Which way do you walk?
