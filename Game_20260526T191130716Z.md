The blue is everything. You drift — no surface above, no floor below, no horizon in any direction. Only the same blue, extending forever in every way you turn. Something is taking shape in you — not yet a name, not yet a story, but a single truth you are holding without knowing why. The hum does not reach here. Nothing reaches here. You are alone, and the blue is patient.

What do you hold?

## Turn 1

**Player:** IAM NOTUM SUM
Mortar. The bridge-builder. The tower-dropper. Show me the footings and I'll show you where they cut corners — then I'll show you how to bring it down clean and teach the town to do it themselves next time.

**Game Master:**

You surface into light the way a diver surfaces — not gently, not by choice, but because your body remembers air even when your mind has forgotten it. The blue shatters. The desert takes you: white sand, red sun, a heat that presses down on your shoulders like a hand. No tumble. No leg injury. The body you wear was earned in a prior life, and it carries no wound save the one you brought with you.

The fountain clears the air before you reach it — the sound of water from somewhere ahead. You follow it. The cobblestones come first: a ring of them around a clearing, fresh and precise, out of place in the sand. Then the fountain itself, and the inscription carved deep into its basin wall — three Latin words, hand-cut and weathered: IAM NOTUM SUM.

He is sitting on the rim. Broad-shouldered, tall even seated. Stone ridge along the spine, stone in the major joints. A face that has the weight of someone who has carried a lot and put none of it down wrong. He has a book open in his hands but his eyes are on you — not suspicious, not welcoming. Testing. The way someone watches a new arrival who is not quite a stranger.

He sets the book down. Closes it. Looks at you across the inscription.

*"Mortar,"* he says, and his voice is plain. *"You come with the words and you come with a name. That's more than most. What do you build?"*

The town waits behind him — four landmarks visible from here, each of them new, none of them quite finished. The question is an invitation. Or a test. With him, it might be both.

What do you answer?
