The blue has no end. It presses from every direction at once — not water, not air, not anything you have a name for. You are aware, but the awareness has no edges. You do not have a body yet; you are the suggestion of a body, the faintest scaffolding, the place where a self might eventually stand. You cling to something you cannot quite see, a single concept half-formed, the only shape in all that formless blue. Somewhere far above there may be something else. Here, there is only the blue, and you inside it, and the quiet conviction that you are more than the blue knows. What do you call yourself, and what do you carry?
