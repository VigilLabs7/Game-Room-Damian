The blue has no end. It presses from every direction at once — not water, not air, not anything you have a name for. You are aware, but the awareness has no edges. You do not have a body yet; you are the suggestion of a body, the faintest scaffolding, the place where a self might eventually stand. You cling to something you cannot quite see, a single concept half-formed, the only shape in all that formless blue. Somewhere far above there may be something else. Here, there is only the blue, and you inside it, and the quiet conviction that you are more than the blue knows. What do you call yourself, and what do you carry?

The words arrive before you chose them. *Tinder.* The syllable costs something — a price in selfhood you did not know you could pay. It lands in the blue and the blue does not swallow it. The ideatum settles. Not taken from you — taken *by* you. What you carry is now what you are. A spark that does not go out. The blue thins at the edges of it, just slightly, as if the nothing around you has begun to acknowledge there is something here worth not consuming.

You are still in the blue. You are still without a body. But the scaffolding has a name now, and the name is yours, and the fire beneath it is the last honest thing in this place. Something above you — you cannot say what — may or may not be listening. Something outside the blue. Something with a voice you will not hear yet.

What do you do with the spark?
