There is no color here. That is the first thing. Not black, not white — absence. The sense of being held in something that has not yet decided what shape to take, and neither have you. You are here but you are not yet a here. There is no floor to push against, no surface to reach for, no air to breathe because there is no breath yet — only the fact of being, still unattached to a body, still waiting for the shape that will make you real.

Somewhere above you — not a direction, more like a meaning — something is calling. Not to you. Not yet. It is calling to what you might become, and you understand that you are going to have to choose what that is. You have time. You have nothing but time. The question is what you bring with you when the call arrives.

What do you do?
