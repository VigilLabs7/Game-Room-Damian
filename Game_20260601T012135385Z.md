You are in the blue.

There is no surface. No floor. No horizon. The same blue in every direction, and you in it — not swimming, not sinking, simply being, without form or weight or name. Something brushes against the edge of what you are. A concept. A single truth you are holding without knowing you are holding anything at all.

The blue has no sound. No current. No others. You are alone here, and the aloneness is not cruel — it is simply the condition. You cannot reach sideways and find company. The blue does not answer when you call.

But something is taking shape. Faintly. A column in water, a ledger finding its rows. You grip it tighter and it clarifies. A principle. A reason to persist. This is what you will bring with you when the blue gives way.

Something calls from above. You may not hear it yet. When you do — when your truth catches the call — you will rise.

**What do you hold?**

The sun is a white point directly above, unmoved. A fountain at the center of a square, water lifting and falling without sound. And on the rim of the basin, mist and form — dark eyes, a face that is still deciding what it wants to be, shoulders that sharpen briefly and then thin again in the heat.

Mortar. The name you gave the blue and the blue gave back to you. The words you carry — *the span that was meant to close and didn't* — arrive with you, settled into your chest like keystones finding their arch.

The figure on the rim watches. The water rises behind them. The inscription on the basin's far side catches the light: IAM NOTUM SUM, carved deep, hand-cut, uneven — the work of someone who meant every letter.

The figure shifts on the rim. Does not stand. Turns toward you with the slow attention of someone who has been waiting.

"Mortar," the figure says. The voice is thin, used more for listening than speaking. "You came back different."

The square is quiet. The crossroads are bare dirt for now. Four landmarks wait at the edges — north, east, south, west — but the fountain is here and the figure is here and the question is not where you go but what you say to the one who was here before you.

The leg is whole. The body is earned.

**What do you say?**
