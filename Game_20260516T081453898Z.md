You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. Becoming less.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at your center — a notion, a phrase, a conviction you cannot let go of. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. The blue presses in. But it holds.

You are clinging to it. A log in the void. A raft. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

Above you — just barely above you — you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A layer where things have weight and names. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. They call it the Thickening.

Others are rising. You can feel them in the deep — other flickers, other notions, clinging to their own logs. Some will make it. Some won't.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

The green is a tree. A single tree, old and wide, sitting at the center of a stone circle where water should be but is not — the stone holds the shape of water the way a mold holds the shape of what was poured into it, and the shape is still here but the water is gone, and sitting on the rim of the fountain is a figure.

"You carry something that answers to old names," he says. His voice is precise. Measured. Each word placed in a column. "The torch bends because it knows what is waiting. The coal burns because it remembers what it was before it was coal. You are the bridge between."

The torch flame bends toward the figure, reading him, and what it reads is: debt. The barkeep is owed. Money-money is owed. The journal in his hands has pages of the same fibrous stock as the dead gomix's — pages that make room for words not yet written.

Above the tree, at the town's edge, you can see the commercial strip. The general store. Morning light off water. The gold thread seam that does not complete. The crack in the counter that has been waiting since before the store was a store.

The figure at the fountain is still watching you.

"What do you carry?" he asks again. "And what are you willing to let burn?"
