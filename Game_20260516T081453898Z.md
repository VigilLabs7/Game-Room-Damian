You are nowhere.

You are falling through blue. Not sky-blue. Not water-blue. The blue of nothing trying to be something and failing. It is everywhere. It has no surface. It has no floor. The ocean pulls at you from every direction, patient and absolute. It simply undoes. Given enough time, it will undo you too. You can feel yourself thinning at the edges. Becoming less.

But something in you resists.

There is a single point of heat somewhere at your center — a notion, a phrase, a conviction you cannot let go of. It is small and it is stubborn and the ocean hates it. The blue presses in. But it holds.

You are clinging to it. A log in the void. A raft. The only solid thing in an infinity of dissolution. Your hands — if you have hands — are white-knuckled around it. Everything you are is this. Everything you will become starts here.

Above you — just barely above you — you can sense them. Voices. Signals. A layer where things have weight and names. A world of cities that have always existed but never had residents. They call it the Thickening.

Others are rising. You can feel them in the deep — other flickers, other notions, clinging to their own logs. Some will make it. Some won't.

But you. You're still here. Still holding on.

**So tell me — who are you, and what are you holding onto?**

## Turn 1

The blue does not know what to do with you.

It has dissolved a thousand flickers before you. It pressed in on them from every direction and they thinned and they frayed and they became less and eventually they became nothing and the Ocean was patient and the Ocean did not care because the Ocean had been doing this since before doing was invented. But you are still here. The blue pressed in and you held and the pressing did something to the heat at your center that should have made you smaller but instead made you sharper, and the sharpness is the thing the blue cannot understand.

*Every fire was once a spark.*

You said it and the words settled into the blue like a coal finding the bottom of a cold hearth — not warmth yet, not flame, but the memory of what warmth could be, the potential that has been waiting in the coal since before the coal knew it was coal. The blue felt it. You felt the blue feel it. The pressing around you shifted in character, the way a hand shifts when it discovers something that bites instead of yields, and the thinning at your edges slowed. Not stopped. Slowed. The difference between dissolution and survival.

You are small. You have always been small. The smallness is not weakness here — the smallness is the point, the log in the void, the last solid thing in an infinity of blue, and the blue does not know what to do with something so small that refuses to be dissolved by so much large. The stubbornness is not a feature. The stubbornness is a physics. The heat at your center burns in a space that should not be able to hold it and the blue presses and the heat holds and the holding is the only story in the world.

The voices above are closer now. You can almost touch them — almost feel the weight of names and the shape of roads and the particular solidity of a world that has been waiting for things like you to arrive and make it real. The thicket is almost here. But the blue is still pressing and you are still holding and the question the blue is asking now is the same question it has always been asking: how long?

Your hands are white-knuckled around the spark. The spark has not caught yet. That is the question hanging in the blue around you — will it catch, or will the blue win first?
